# Lecture Spec — Underdogs Education Planning (1N2D Hackathon)

> Unified specification for English lecture deck + script + 5 country assignments.

## Audience & Goal

**Audience**: Underdogs external coaches + planning team (English-speaking), plus partners in Indonesia / Korea / Japan / Taiwan / India.

**Goal**: Each participant/coach finishes the workshop able to **design and run a 1-night-2-day hackathon** that (a) reflects Underdogs' education planning philosophy, (b) localizes to the country context, and (c) connects pre-event LMS/VOD preparation with post-event follow-up.

**Deliverable language**: English (with local-language appendices where noted).

## 5D Framework (Underdogs ACTT Extension)

> Source: ACTT_5D_Framework_v1.0.pdf. These are the 5 competencies we measure in participants.

| Code | Name | Meaning | Observable signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **D1** | Desire | Why do you want this? | Clear personal stake / story |
| **D2** | Direction | Where are you going? | Specific goal + near-term milestone |
| **D3** | Discipline | How do you execute? | Habit + cadence + self-monitoring |
| **D4** | Design | How do you prototype? | Ability to test assumption with MVP |
| **D5** | Durability | How do you sustain? | Resilience + learning loop + team |

These map cleanly to Impact Value Chain:
- Desire + Direction → feed the **Outcome / Impact** (the "why")
- Discipline + Design → feed the **Activity / Output** (the "how")
- Durability → feeds the **long-term Impact**

## Country Track Design Data

### 🇮🇩 Indonesia (BSD City)

- **Local anchor**: BSD City (Tangerang) — Indonesia's "Living Lab", partner Sinarmas Land + 7 universities (Monash Indonesia, Prasmul, BINUS...)
- **6 impact tracks** (teams choose 1): Urban Mobility · Health & Biomedical · Green & Sustainability · Digital Inclusion · UMKM/Local Economy · Education Access
- **Operating mode**: Bilingual EN + Bahasa Indonesia
- **Pre-education**: 2-3 weeks async (mandatory per partner brief)
- **PoC discipline**: "Maximum Validation with Minimum Resources"
- **Methodology mix**: IMPACT + 로컬 adaptation
- **Funnel**: Stage 1 (1N2D hackathon) → Stage 2 (3-4 month batch) → Stage 3 (alliance/fund)
- **Coach-local partner boundary**: must be clearly defined

### 🇰🇷 Korea — Inbound (International students in Korea)

- **Target**: Foreign university students in Korean universities, wanting to start up IN Korea
- **Core challenge**: K-market localization (language, culture, regulatory environment, funding access)
- **Theme**: "K-Market Entry for Foreign Founders"
- **Methodology**: IMPACT + 소상공인 성장 (for B2C/local) OR 글로벌진출 (reversed — they're the "global" founders entering KR)
- **Pre-education**: Korean startup ecosystem basics, KOREA-VISA for founders, TIPS/KISED programs
- **Special resources**: Korean mentor pool, alumni founders who are foreign-origin

### 🇰🇷 Korea — Outbound (Korean teams going abroad)

- **Target**: Korean teams looking at Japan / SE Asia / India / US expansion
- **Core challenge**: Target-market discovery, local partner mapping, cross-cultural team
- **Theme**: "Born Global from Day 1"
- **Methodology**: 글로벌진출
- **Pre-education**: Target-market primers (Japan, Indonesia, India — based on teams' interest)
- **Special resources**: Underdogs Japan / India branches, 520+ global partner network

### 🇯🇵🇹🇼 Japan + Taiwan (East Asian Social Problems)

- **Local anchor**: Musashino Univ. 앙트프레너십학부 (JP) + 台中 社會創新實驗基地 (TW)
- **Problem pool** (curated, teams choose 1):
  - 고령화 (Aging society)
  - 환경 (Environment)
  - 지역소멸 (Regional depopulation)
  - 기후변화 (Climate change)
- **Methodology**: 매칭 + 커스텀 (social-enterprise oriented)
- **Judging (4 axes, from SU Team)**: Idea specificity · BM completeness · Growth potential · Impact (market + socio-economic ripple)
- **Coach cadence**: 3-4 sessions per team (from SU Team EASI LEAGUE pattern)
- **Deliverable standard**: BM with validation plan attached

### 🇮🇳 India (Action AI — Fin-Act + AI Literacy)

- **Program line**: Action AI — currently sold to universities as Fin-Act and AI Literacy tracks
- **Philosophy**: Build many entrepreneurs at scale, not few unicorns. One 5D competency per 1N2D (can't install all 5 in 36 hours).
- **Chosen 5D focus**: **Design** (MVP prototyping) — most teachable in 36 hours with AI tools
- **Action AI track pool** (teams pick 1 from 6): Access & Regional Divide · AI × Senior Life · Industrial Transformation · Everyday Safety · Climate & Circular · Social Cohesion & Open
- **Operating mode**: "Not a course, a launchpad" — live product URL on Day 1 end
- **AI tooling**: Gemini · Lovable · Claude Code (all free tier)
- **Performance progression**: 20,000 → 1,000 → 100 → 10 finalists; Korea Grand Showcase for top finalists

## Assignment Structure (same skeleton across all 5 tracks)

```
A. Pre-event (2-3 weeks before the hackathon)
   A1. LMS video sequence (5-8 videos, 10-15min each)
   A2. Reading kit (3-5 short readings)
   A3. Pre-event individual exercise (due before arrival)
   A4. Team formation hint (optional — some tracks pre-form teams)

B. Day 1 (in-person)
   B1. 09:00-10:00 Opening + Icebreaker
   B2. 10:00-12:00 Problem deep-dive (track-specific)
   B3. 13:00-15:00 Team formation + Ideation
   B4. 15:00-18:00 MVP sprint #1 with 1:1 coaching
   B5. 19:00-22:00 Evening sprint + social bonding (dinner + informal coaching)

C. Day 2 (in-person)
   C1. 08:00-10:00 MVP sprint #2 (polish + demo prep)
   C2. 10:00-12:00 Dry-run + coach feedback
   C3. 13:00-15:00 Demo Day (5-min pitch + Q&A)
   C4. 15:00-16:00 Judging + awards
   C5. 16:00-17:00 Next-steps briefing (Stage 2 batch onboarding)

D. Post-event (follow-up window 2-8 weeks)
   D1. Written report (1-pager per team)
   D2. Stage 2 batch onboarding (if selected)
   D3. Alumni network + quarterly check-in
```

## Engagement Techniques (to emphasize in Part C)

1. **Milestone compression** — each 2-hour block has a visible deliverable
2. **1:1 coaching density** — 3-4 sessions per team, 20-30 min each
3. **Peer accountability** — public daily standup (2 min per team)
4. **Immersive environment** — dinner together, optional overnight on-site
5. **Physical artifact** — every team produces a tangible output (board, poster, live URL)
6. **Public demo** — external judges, not just internal coaches
7. **Celebration** — award ceremony, group photo, post-event social

## Assessment Rubric (from SU Team, adopted)

| Axis | Weight | What we look for |
|---|---|---|
| **Idea specificity** | 25% | Is the problem concrete? Is the insight non-obvious? |
| **BM completeness** | 25% | Who pays, how much, via what channel? Unit economics? |
| **Growth potential** | 25% | Can this 10x? What's the wedge + expansion path? |
| **Impact** | 25% | Market size × social/economic ripple. Tied to Outcome. |

Jury: 3 people = 1 public-sector expert + 1 private-sector expert + 1 Underdogs coach.
