# Assignment 03 — Korea Outbound (Born Global from Day 1)

> **Track**: Korean founders/teams targeting Japan · Indonesia · India · USA expansion
> **Theme**: Born Global from Day 1
> **Methodology**: 글로벌진출 (Born Global)
> **5D Anchor**: **Direction + Durability** — teams must commit to a cross-border destination early (Direction) and design a team + roadmap that survives a 12-24 month learning cycle in a market they do not intuit (Durability).
> **Format**: 2-3 weeks pre-event LMS + 1N2D in-person + 2-8 weeks post-event follow-up
> **Team size**: 3-4 people, mixed-skill (Business + Tech + Design encouraged)
> **Assessment**: 4 axes x 25% — Idea · BM · Growth · Impact (with "Cross-border readiness" as a visible lens inside BM and Growth)

---

## Why these 2 D's, not others

Cross-border expansion fails not from lack of desire or prototyping skill, but because teams (1) commit to a market for the wrong reasons and drift between 3 countries for 18 months, and (2) burn out in month 9 when the first local hire quits. Direction pins down "where and why"; Durability funds "how to still be standing in 2 years". Scoring weight and coach attention tilt toward D2 + D5.

### A2. Reading kit (3 short reads, ~30 min total)

1. **520+ Global Partner Network — directory excerpt** (Underdogs internal) — which partners cover JP / ID / IN / US, what they do, how to request intro.
2. **LICORN screening criteria** — what Underdogs' in-house Lifestyle & Local Innovation uniCORN fund looks for in Born-Global teams (ticket size, stage, sector fit).
3. **"Born Global from Day 1" one-pager** — the 6 decisions every Born-Global founder makes in the first 90 days.

### A3. Pre-event individual exercise (due before arrival)

**Title: "Pick your target market + 3 hypotheses about a customer you've never met"**

Each participant submits a 1-page PDF to the LMS containing:

1. **Target market pick** — exactly 1 of: Japan · Indonesia · India · USA. One paragraph on *why this market, not the other three*.
2. **Three hypotheses** about the customer in that market, each in the form:
   - *"I believe [customer segment] in [target market] currently [behavior / pain], and would pay for [solution] because [belief]. I am wrong if [falsification condition]."*
3. **Two unknowns** — two specific things the participant does *not* know about that customer and must find out during the 1N2D.

> Coaches review all submissions in the week before the event. Flag any participant whose hypotheses are actually about the Korean market in disguise — a common trap.

### A4. Team formation hint

Teams are **not** pre-formed. On Day 1 morning, participants cluster by target market first, then by complementary skills (business / tech / design) within the market cluster. Ideal: 3-4 per team, at least 2 of the 3 skill profiles covered, at least 1 member with prior exposure to the target market (language, study abroad, internship, family connection).

- Welcome + workshop goal (Direction + Durability, not MVP polish).
- **Icebreaker: "Your passport moment"** — each participant shares a 60-second story of the moment they decided Korea wasn't enough.
- Facilitator posts the 4 target-market corners (JP / ID / IN / US) around the room. Participants physically walk to their pre-submitted pick.

### B2. 10:00-12:00 — Problem deep-dive (target-market framing)

- Country-cluster breakouts. Each cluster has a **[LOCAL COACH TO FILL IN — regional expert coach per country, ideally Underdogs JP / IN branch staff or 520+ partner rep]** walking the cluster through:
  - 3 "obvious ideas that fail" in that market, with the reason.
  - 3 "non-obvious wedges" Korean founders have actually won on.
  - The 2 questions the cluster must answer before noon tomorrow.
- Deliverable by 12:00: each team drafts a 3-bullet **Market-Entry Thesis** on one A3 sheet.

### B3. 13:00-15:00 — Team formation + Ideation

- Team formation finalized (3-4 people, market-cluster-bound).
- Each team writes a **Problem Statement Card**: *Who* (segment), *Where* (market), *What* (pain), *Why now* (trigger), *Why us* (unfair advantage).
- 30-min "hypothesis stress test" — teams swap cards with a neighboring team and attack each other's assumptions for 15 min each side.

### D2. Soft-landing pathway (Weeks 2-4)

- **520+ partner intro** — each team gets up to 2 warm intros from the Underdogs global partner network, tied to their partner-mapping deliverable.

## E. Target-Market Discovery Playbook (how to interview a customer you've never met, in 1N2D)

**Constraint**: you cannot fly there. You have ~6 working hours across the two days. Use async + remote calls.

### The 5-step remote interview flow

1. **Warm intro path** — ask Underdogs 520+ coordinator at 15:00 Day 1 for 1-2 contacts in your segment. Do not cold-email during the event; conversion is too low.
2. **Pre-brief the interviewee in their language** — send a 3-line context message in Japanese / Bahasa / Hindi / English (not Korean). Template in Appendix A.
3. **30-minute structured call** using the template below.
4. **Capture the quote** — write down 2 verbatim sentences. These become pitch ammunition.
5. **Re-contact within 24h** — thank-you + 1 follow-up question. This tests Durability of relationship.

### Sample questions (cultural-safe, non-leading)

- *"Walk me through the last time you faced [problem]. What did you actually do?"* — behavioral, not hypothetical.
- *"Who else was involved in that decision?"* — uncovers the real buying center (critical in JP B2B).
- *"What would have to be true for you to switch from your current solution?"* — surfaces the falsification condition.
- *"Is there anyone else I should talk to?"* — expands network at zero cost.
- **Avoid**: "Would you use our product?" "Do you think this is a good idea?" "How much would you pay?"

## F. Cross-Cultural Team Health — 5 coach check-in prompts

Coaches run these during each 1:1 block. Not all at once — pick 1-2 per session.

1. **"Whose voice has been quietest in the last hour, and why?"** — surfaces whether the Korean-native default is dominating a member with target-market experience.
2. **"What's a word or phrase someone on the team used today that didn't quite land?"** — unpacks language/cultural friction early.
3. **"If your target-market customer were in this room right now, what would they correct you on?"** — forces empathy calibration.
4. **"Who on the team is carrying the Durability weight — the 12-month thinking? Is that sustainable?"** — detects burnout concentration.
5. **"What's one decision you've made in the last 3 hours that you'd reverse if you had a native [target-market] advisor in the room?"** — licenses humility.

## H. Coach Cue Cards — 5 prompts for difficult moments

### Card 1 — "Team has had zero international customer contact by Day 1 dinner"
Ask: *"What's the smallest conversation you could have in the next 90 minutes with one real person in your target market? Let's list 3 paths — 520+ intro, LinkedIn warm contact, or an international student from your alma mater. Pick one. Go."*
Do not accept "we'll do secondary research instead."

### Card 2 — "Team picked too big a market (e.g. 'all of SE Asia' or 'all of USA')"
Ask: *"Name the specific city, the specific buyer title, and the specific use-case. If you can't name all three, you've picked the wrong market — you've picked a region."*
Push them to collapse to one city (Jakarta / Mumbai / Tokyo / Brooklyn) for the duration of the 1N2D.

### Card 3 — "Team is culturally under-prepared (making assumptions that don't travel)"
Ask: *"Let's replay the last 10 minutes as if our target-market customer was in the room. What would they have flinched at?"*
Then route the team to the on-site branch coach for a 15-min cultural calibration.

### Card 4 — "Team is arguing about which market to pick, 3 hours in"
Ask: *"You have two options: commit to one now and discover you were wrong in 4 hours, or keep debating and discover you've shipped nothing in 24. Which is the cheaper mistake?"*
Force a commit by 15:00. Document the losing market as "Market #2 hypothesis for post-event."

### Card 5 — "Team has a great MVP but no Durability plan"
Ask: *"Imagine it's month 9 and your first local hire just quit. What's your Tuesday morning like? Who's still on the team, and who owns what?"*
Durability is 50% of why Korean Outbound teams fail — this is non-negotiable by Day 2 pitch.
