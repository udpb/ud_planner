# 02 — Korea Inbound Hackathon

**Theme**: "K-Market Entry for Foreign Founders"
**Audience**: International university students enrolled in Korean universities (undergrad / grad / exchange) who want to start up IN Korea
**Format**: 1-Night-2-Day intensive hackathon + 2–3 weeks pre-event LMS + 2–8 weeks post-event follow-up
**Language**: English (Korean terms inline with Romanization + meaning)
**Methodology mix**: IMPACT + 소상공인 성장 (*sosanggongin seongjang*, SME / local B2C growth) OR 글로벌진출 (*global jinchul*, global expansion — reversed, they enter KR from abroad)
**5D anchors (pick 2)**: **D2 Direction** + **D4 Design**
**Why these two Ds**: Foreign founders arrive with either (a) vague "I want to build in Korea" urge, or (b) a home-country idea to port. Both fail at the same joints — **Direction** (no concrete, localized goal) and **Design** (can't test assumptions fast against Korean users). Desire is already present (they moved countries). Discipline and Durability are Stage-2 concerns. 36 hours is best spent sharpening near-term goal + shipping one MVP test against real Korean users.

11:30–12:00: IMPACT chain exercise — each team sketches I→A→O→Oc→Im on a worksheet for their track.

### B3. 13:00–12:00 [sic] → 13:00–15:00 — Team formation lock + Ideation

- Korean Buddies join at 13:00. 15 min speed intros.

### B4. 15:00–18:00 — MVP sprint #1 + 1:1 coaching

- **D4 Design exercise**: build the leanest possible test of the top-ranked assumption. Options allowed: landing page (Lovable / Framer free tier), paper prototype + Korean-language user interview script, WhatsApp/KakaoTalk chatbot mock, pre-order form
- Coach rotation #2 (20 min per team, ~17:00)
- 18:00 checkpoint: every team has one **testable artifact in Korean** (Buddy translates)

### B5. 19:00–22:00 — Evening sprint + social bonding

- 19:00–20:00 team dinner (Korean food sampler — intentional cultural immersion, 회식 *hoesik* light version, no alcohol pressure)
- 20:00–22:00 field test window: teams hit nearby areas (대학로 Daehak-ro, 신촌 Sinchon, 홍대 Hongdae — whichever is nearest) with their artifact and **collect ≥10 real Korean-user data points**
- 22:00 debrief standup: 2 min per team — *What did we learn? What will we change tomorrow?*
- Optional overnight on-site workspace until 24:00

## Legal & Visa primer (concise)

**D-8-4 Startup Visa** — for foreigners who have (a) a bachelor's degree or higher, OR (b) recognized IP / awards, AND (c) pass OASIS point system (80+ points of 120). No minimum capital required for D-8-4 (unlike D-8-1). Valid up to 2 years, renewable.

**D-10-2 Startup Job-Seeker Visa** — 6 months (extendable to 2 years) for foreigners preparing to launch. Allows participation in accelerator / gov programs like K-Startup Grand Challenge. Good "bridge" visa.

**Business registration steps (simplified)**: (1) Choose entity — 주식회사 *jusik-hoesa* (Ltd. Co.) vs 개인사업자 *gaein-saeopja* (sole proprietor). (2) Register at tax office (*세무서 semuseo*) — foreigners need Alien Registration Card first. (3) Open corporate bank account — hardest step for foreigners, allow 2–4 weeks. (4) E-commerce license if selling online (통신판매업 *tongsin-panmae-eop*).

**Funding access**: TIPS (~KRW 500M seed, requires accredited investor nomination), K-Startup Grand Challenge (KRW 40M + accelerator, foreign-founder-specific), Seoul Global Startup Center (free office + mentoring).

## Language & cultural survival — 10 business-culture tips

1. **Business card (*명함 myeongham*)** — receive with two hands, read it, place on table during meeting. Never pocket immediately.
2. **Bowing** — small bow (15°) when greeting business contacts. Handshake + bow combo is fine.
3. **Hierarchy** — greet the most senior person first. Age matters; 선배 *seonbae* / 후배 *hubae* dynamic is real even in startups.
4. **Evening dinner (*회식 hoesik*)** — expect work dinners. You can decline alcohol politely ("죄송해요, 술을 못 마셔요"). Building trust happens here, not in the meeting room.
5. **Decision speed** — initial meetings feel slow (trust-building). Once trust is built, execution is fast. Don't push for decisions in meeting #1.
6. **Pay tab etiquette** — senior usually pays. As a foreigner, offer but don't insist.
7. **"Let me check internally"** — often a polite no. Read between lines.
8. **KakaoTalk (*카카오톡*) is business infrastructure** — get on it Day 1. Business cards should include your KakaoTalk ID.
9. **Contracts** — verbal agreements carry weight but always follow with written email or KakaoTalk screenshot. *"말로 한 것도 계약이다"* but documentation still matters.
10. **Respect for the product** — bring a small gift (fruit, coffee) to a first in-person meeting at a partner office. Presentation of the gift matters.

## Track-specific coaching prompts (3 per track)

### Track 1 — K-Food / K-Beauty export assist
- "Which specific Korean brand have you talked to? Not 'I'll talk to', but 'I talked to last week'."
- "Your advantage is the foreign side. What *exactly* do you know about your home-country buyer that a Korean exporter doesn't?"
- "Regulatory — do you know KC certification vs FDA vs CE for your category? If no, that's your next 2-hour task."

### Track 2 — EdTech for foreigners learning Korean
- "TOPIK has existed since 1997. What have incumbents missed that you noticed because you lived it?"
- "Who pays — the learner, the university, or an employer? Show me three real conversations."
- "What's the wedge? You can't beat 듀오링고 *Duolingo* on scale. What specific slice of the journey are you owning?"

### Track 3 — Tourism tech
- "Walk me through a real tourist's 72-hour Seoul trip. Where exactly does your product enter their day?"
- "Foreigner-pain-point vs Korean-pain-point — is this a pain only foreigners feel, or a universal pain with a language-layer fix?"
- "Seasonality — what's your play in 비수기 *bisugi* (off-season)? Tourism revenue is lumpy."

### Track 4 — B2B SaaS for Korean SMEs
- "소상공인 *sosanggongin* are famously hard to sell SaaS to. What's your channel — direct, association partnership, or platform bundle?"
- "Show me one real 사장님 *sajangnim* (shop owner) you've talked to this week. What did they actually say — not what you hoped they'd say?"
- "Pricing — KRW 9,900/month, KRW 29,000/month, or revenue share? Why?"

## Coach cue cards (5 scripts) — with foreign-founder sensitivity

**Cue 1 — When they blame the language barrier**
> "Language is real, I won't dismiss it. But right now, at this stage, which 3 conversations would crack this open? Let's see if your Korean Buddy or 1 coach intro can unblock you in the next 2 hours. Name the 3 people."

**Cue 2 — When visa uncertainty is freezing their ambition**
> "Visa is a solvable problem, not a permission slip you wait for. The D-10-2 bridge is designed for exactly this. What you build this weekend makes your visa case stronger, not the reverse. Build first, paperwork second."

**Cue 3 — When they say "Korean consumers don't understand my product"**
> "That's feedback, not a verdict. Who *specifically* said that, and what did they want instead? If you can't name 5 Koreans you've shown this to, the answer is more conversations, not a better pitch."

**Cue 4 — When capital access feels impossible**
> "You don't need capital today. You need one paying customer or one signed pilot. Capital follows evidence. What's the smallest paid pilot you could close in 30 days?"

**Cue 5 — When they're copy-pasting a home-country idea**
> "This worked in [home country]. What's the one Korean insight that makes this *better here than there*, not just *also here*? If you can't answer, we might have the wrong idea on the table."

## Assessment rubric (4 axes × 25% each, with Localization Fit as visible lens)

| Axis | Weight | Primary question | Localization-fit lens |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Idea specificity** | 25% | Is the problem concrete? Is the insight non-obvious? | Does the insight require *Korean-context* knowledge, or is it a generic global insight? Score higher for the former. |
| **BM completeness** | 25% | Who pays, how much, via what channel? Unit economics? | Is the payment channel realistic for KR (KakaoPay / Toss / 무통장입금 *mutongjang ipgeum* bank transfer)? Is pricing in KRW with a KR-consumer-psychology-aware number? |
| **Growth potential** | 25% | Can this 10x? What's the wedge + expansion path? | Is the KR wedge credible *and* is there a plausible bridge to the founder's home-country diaspora or market (a foreigner-founder advantage)? |
| **Impact** | 25% | Market size × social/economic ripple. Tied to Outcome. | Does the product create bridge value — Koreans benefit AND foreigners benefit? (Bi-directional impact = higher score.) |

**Localization Fit is not a 5th axis — it's the lens applied to all four.** Teams that build globally-generic ideas without KR specificity cap at ~60% on each axis regardless of polish.
