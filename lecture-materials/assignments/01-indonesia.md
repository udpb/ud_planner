# Assignment 01 — Indonesia (BSD City) 1-Night-2-Day Hackathon

> Facilitator's pack for Underdogs external coaches running the **BSD City-preneurship PoC (Stage 1)** in bilingual EN + Bahasa Indonesia mode.
> Anchor partner: **Sinarmas Land + Living Lab Ventures (LLV)** and 7 BSD-area universities (Monash Indonesia, Prasmul, Atma Jaya BSD, BINUS, ITTS, BSI, UIC College BSD).
> Compressed from the UCA4 3D2N brief — roughly 33% of block-time has been dropped; Day 1 deep-dive and Day 2 ideation are merged into a single overnight push.

---

## 0. Design Anchors

- **5D emphasis — Desire + Design.** *Desire* because BSD is a 500k-resident real city; teams need a personal stake or the pitch becomes a case study. *Design* because 24 hours only permits MVP prototyping. Direction/Discipline/Durability are signaled, not assessed here.
- **PoC discipline — "Maximum Validation with Minimum Resources."** Every MVP must name (a) cheapest falsification test, (b) spend to run it, (c) what was learned in 24 hours.
- **Funnel visibility.** Top 3-5 teams enter Stage 2 (3-4 month batch) → Stage 3 (BSD Alliance + fund). Teams hear this at hour one.
- **Language rule.** Opening, coaching, judge Q&A = **EN-primary + BI mirror**. Team internal work = team's choice. Deliverables = **EN required, BI optional**.

## B. Day 1 — In-person, BSD City

### B1 · 09:00–10:00 — Opening + Icebreaker (Lang: EN-primary + BI mirror)

- Sinarmas Land welcome (5 min, BI).
- Program overview by lead coach (15 min, EN): funnel, 6 tracks, PoC discipline, rubric, Stage 2 slots.
- **Icebreaker — "Masalah kota saya" / "My city's problem"** (30 min): each participant says *"Saya [name], dari [university], dan masalah yang saya benci di BSD adalah..."* — surfaces Desire before teams form.
- Logistics (10 min).

### B2 · 10:00–12:00 — Problem Deep-Dive (Lang: bilingual)

- **45 min — Track panels** (local-partner-owned): 5-min expert per track (Green Habit lead, UMKM founder, Apple Academy rep, etc.).
- **45 min — Field micro-interview.** Teams do 2 × 10-min interviews in BSD (mall, kampung boundary, cafeteria). Worksheet: *problem / frequency / workaround / would you pay*.
- **30 min — Return + cluster insights on track sticky-boards.**

> Coach = facilitate "So What?". Local partner = source panelists + escort to kampung/UMKM.

### B3 · 13:00–15:00 — Team Formation + Ideation (Lang: EN-primary)

- 30 min team formation at track-boards (coaches cap 2-3 teams/track).
- 45 min "How Might We" — 3 HMW → pick 1.
- 45 min 3 divergent solutions → narrow via cost/impact 2x2.

**Deliverable:** filled **Problem-Solution Canvas** (A3 poster, pre-printed).

### B4 · 15:00–18:00 — MVP Sprint #1 + 1:1 Coaching (Lang: team's choice)

- Teams build. Coaches run **20-min 1:1 per team** (6 teams × 20 min rotation).
- Every MVP backlog must list *the cheapest falsification test* (coach enforces via Cue Cards).
- By 18:00: clickable/paper prototype + test script for one interview.

> MVP forms: Figma · paper storyboard · WhatsApp bot · poster + role-play. Actual code = discouraged (time trap).

### B5 · 19:00–22:00 — Evening Sprint + Social Bonding (Lang: bilingual, relaxed)

- 19:00–20:00 group dinner, mixed seating (name cards, no team tables).
- 20:00–21:30 evening sprint: 1 more customer interview, update prototype.
- 21:30–22:00 "2-min team standup": *problem / who we talked to / what changed / what we still don't know*.

Coaches off-duty 22:00–08:00 (enforced).

## 8. Localization Notes

- **UMKM** = *Usaha Mikro, Kecil, dan Menengah* (Micro/Small/Medium Enterprises). Indonesia has ~65.5M UMKMs; politically and economically the most resonant track.
- **BSD City** = Bumi Serpong Damai, Tangerang. 10,000 ha, 500k residents, Indonesia's "Living Lab." Key zones: Green Office Park, The Breeze, Apple Developer Academy, Monash Indonesia, Prasmul.
- **KEK ETKI** = biomedical special economic zone (Health & Biomedical track).
- **Kampung** ≈ urban village; the richest "real problem" source for field interviews — Sinarmas Land can facilitate access.
- **Greetings.** Open plenary with *"Selamat pagi, apa kabar?"* — reply *"Baik, terima kasih."* Attempt is respected even without fluency.
- **Hierarchy.** Address senior local partners as *Bapak* / *Ibu [name]*. First-name default is read as rude.
- **Religion/food.** Halal by default; no pork/alcohol. Friday 11:30–13:00 = no hard block (prayer).
- **Pace.** Teams may be quiet in plenary, vocal in small-group. Silence ≠ disengagement; use 1:1 and small-circle coaching.

## 9. Track-Specific Coaching Prompts (for B4 1:1 sessions)

For each track, 3 questions a coach should ask during the 20-min 1:1.

### Urban Mobility
1. Who physically moves differently because of your solution — and how often per week?
2. What's the cheapest test: a WhatsApp broadcast, a taped-off bus stop, or a Google Form?
3. If Sinarmas Land said "pilot this in 1 kampung next month," what would you need?

### Health & Biomedical
1. Are you solving a *medical* problem or a *behavior* problem? (Most student teams confuse these.)
2. Who are the three gatekeepers (regulator, clinician, payer) — and which one did you already call?
3. Could you test the behavior change with a WhatsApp nudge before building anything?

### Green & Sustainability
1. What ton/kg/unit of waste or emission are you measurably changing — and over what period?
2. Who pays today for the problem *not* being solved? That's likely your customer.
3. How does BSD's Green Habit program (since 2017) already partially solve this, and where's your wedge?

### Digital Inclusion
1. Who is *excluded* — not by tech access, but by confidence, language, or trust?
2. Can you prototype with SMS or a WhatsApp bot before assuming app adoption?
3. What would Apple Academy or Purwadhika say about your assumption in one sentence?

### UMKM / Local Economy
1. Walk me through one UMKM owner's day, hour by hour. Which hour are you changing?
2. What's your unit economics *per warung / per owner* at 10, 100, 1000 UMKMs?
3. Are you B2B (sell to UMKM) or B2B2C (use UMKM as channel to their customers)? Pick one.

### Education Access
1. What does a student *do* after your solution that they couldn't do before — observable, not "learn better"?
2. Is the bottleneck content, motivation, language, or credential? Different bottlenecks = different products.
3. Who currently pays for education here — parent, government, employer, student? Your BM lives there.

## 11. Coach Cue Cards — Scripted Prompts for Difficult Moments

**Cue Card #1 — Team is stuck, circling.**
> *"Stop building for 5 minutes. Tell me in one sentence who your user is, and one specific thing they did yesterday that proves your problem is real. If you can't, go interview one more person right now."*

**Cue Card #2 — Team is fighting (usually ideation stage).**
> *"Both ideas might be right. Write each on a sticky note. For each one, answer three questions in 10 minutes: (1) cheapest test, (2) who do we call, (3) what would kill it? Whichever note has better answers, we go with — no vote, the evidence picks."*

**Cue Card #3 — Team is overambitious ("we'll build an AI platform for all 500k residents").**
> *"Beautiful. Now: for the next 18 hours, pretend you have exactly 10 users. Name them. What do those 10 people do on Monday morning that your product touches? Build for those 10. The 500k comes in Stage 3."*

**Cue Card #4 — Team is too quiet / one voice dominates.**
> (To the group, without calling out the dominant voice) *"I want to hear from each person, 60 seconds each, on this one question: what's the assumption that, if wrong, kills this whole idea? No interrupting. I'll time."*

**Cue Card #5 — Team has a "perfect" idea but zero validation.**
> *"Great. I'm the customer. Sell it to me right now, for 200,000 rupiah (~$12). ... OK, what did my face tell you? Now go find 3 more of me in the next 45 minutes. Don't come back without notes."*
