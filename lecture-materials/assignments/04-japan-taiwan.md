# Assignment 04 — Japan + Taiwan 1N2D Hackathon

**Theme**: *East Asian Social Problems — from Insight to MVP in 36 Hours*

**Local anchors**
- Japan — 武蔵野大学 アントレプレナーシップ学部 (Musashino University, Faculty of Entrepreneurship, Tokyo)
- Taiwan — 台中 社會創新實驗基地 (Taichung Social Innovation Lab — 7,000+ innovation-education participants, 350+ incubated teams, 60+ award-winning teams)

**Methodology mix**: 매칭 (Matching) + 커스텀 (Custom, social-enterprise oriented). *Not* IMPACT-default — teams here are building social ventures, so we match them to curated problems and coach around their own business-model shape.

**5D anchors for this track**: **D1 Desire** + **D5 Durability**.
*Rationale*: Social entrepreneurship in East Asia demands (a) a deeply personal "why" that survives slow government cycles and long stakeholder rituals (Desire), and (b) a resilience posture measured in years, not sprints, because regional-depopulation, aging, and climate problems resolve on generational horizons (Durability). Discipline/Design are still assessed in the rubric, but coaches are instructed to probe Desire + Durability first.

**Operating model**: Japan cohort and Taiwan cohort run the **same Day-1/Day-2 skeleton in parallel**. Pre-event is shared. Demo Days run in-country (Tokyo and Taichung) on the same date, with a joint 30-minute video-linked showcase at the end of C3 where one top team per country pitches to the other country's jury.

**Language**: coach-facing materials English. Participant materials localized — Japanese for JP cohort, Traditional Chinese for TW cohort. Translation-required items are flagged `[LOCAL COACH TO FILL IN]`.

## B. Day 1 (in-person, identical timeline JP & TW)

### B1. 09:00–10:00 — Opening + Icebreaker
- Local-partner welcome: Musashino Entrepreneurship Faculty dean (JP) / Taichung SI Lab director (TW). `[LOCAL COACH TO FILL IN — speaker name]`
- Jury introductions (3 jurors per country: 1 public-sector + 1 private-sector + 1 Underdogs coach).
- "Desire Wall" — every participant posts their 500-word statement headline on a physical wall, reads two others. 20 min.

### B2. 10:00–12:00 — Problem deep-dive (track-specific)
- Rotate through 4 problem stations (25 min each + 15 min synthesis):
  Aging · Environment · Regional depopulation · Climate.
- Each station has a **JP data wall** and a **TW data wall** side by side — participants physically see both contexts. This is the visual hook of the track.
- Each participant commits to a **top-2 problem preference**.

### B3. 13:00–15:00 — Team formation + Ideation
- Coach-facilitated matching using Desire Statements + top-2 problem preferences. Target: 4-person teams, each team picks **one curated problem**.
- Ideation sprint: each team produces an *Insight Card* (one-pager): problem framing · target segment · proposed wedge.
- Deliverable by 15:00: Insight Card pinned to team board.

### B4. 15:00–18:00 — MVP sprint #1 + 1:1 coaching (session 1 of 3–4)
- Each team gets a 25-min 1:1 with a coach (one coach handles 2 teams).
- Focus of session 1: Desire-check + problem sharpness. Coach prompt: *"If someone gave you ¥1M / NT$300K tomorrow, what would you spend it on first, and why that?"*
- Teams produce a *Problem-Segment-Solution* triangle by 18:00.

### B5. 19:00–22:00 — Evening sprint + social bonding
- Dinner together on-site (coaches present).
- Informal coaching tables during dinner.
- 20:00–22:00 focused work block: teams draft BM v0.1 canvas (Customer / Value / Channel / Revenue / Cost). Deliverable pinned before lights-down.

## 1. Problem Briefs (curated pool of 4)

Each brief ~80 words English framing + 3 JP + 3 TW local data points. Participant-facing versions are translated into Japanese / Traditional Chinese. Data points below are indicative and must be refreshed yearly — mark latest sources per country.  `[LOCAL COACH TO FILL IN — refresh cite + year]` applies to every data line.

### Problem 1 — Aging society (高齢化 / 高齡化)
East Asia is aging faster than any region in history. Ventures here work on elder care delivery, inter-generational cohousing, workforce reintegration of 60+ workers, and digital access for seniors. Teams are asked to pick one concrete segment (e.g., rural single-household elders, or elder care workers themselves) rather than "seniors in general."

- JP-1 29.1% of Japan is 65+ (2024, highest share on record).
- JP-2 Projected shortage of ~570,000 care workers by 2040.
- JP-3 ~9 million single-elder households nationwide.
- TW-1 Taiwan crossed super-aged society threshold (20%+ at age 65+) in 2025.
- TW-2 Long-Term Care 2.0 budget exceeded NT$80B/year.
- TW-3 Rural townships (e.g., Yunlin, Chiayi) show 65+ shares approaching 25%.

### Problem 2 — Environment (環境)
Includes circular economy, plastic reduction, food waste, water, and urban ecology. Social-venture angle emphasizes behavior-change channels (consumer, school, municipal) over pure greentech. Teams must name **who pays** — municipal procurement, corporate ESG, or B2C premium — early.

- JP-1 Food loss ~5.2M tons/year (household + commercial).
- JP-2 Plastic packaging per capita among highest in OECD.
- JP-3 Municipal recycling rates plateauing despite decades of sorting culture.
- TW-1 Plastic bag / straw restrictions in force since 2019, compliance uneven.
- TW-2 Food-waste volume ~3.6M tons/year across the island.
- TW-3 Taichung city operates one of Asia's most cited circular-economy pilots.

### Problem 3 — Regional depopulation (地方消滅 / 地方消亡)
Shrinking inland towns, vacant-house crises, and thinning local services. Ventures here work on migration attraction, remote-work hubs, akiya / 空屋 reuse, and local-product commerce. Insight axis is critical — teams must prove they understand a **specific place**, not "rural Japan" or "non-Taipei Taiwan."

- JP-1 "Regions at risk of disappearance" list (2024 update) flagged 744 municipalities.
- JP-2 Vacant homes (akiya) reached ~9.0 million (2023 Housing & Land Survey).
- JP-3 Net migration into Tokyo metro still positive every year except 2020.
- TW-1 12 of 22 cities/counties recorded natural population decline in 2024.
- TW-2 Non-six-metro counties captured <10% of net internal migration in the last decade.
- TW-3 Taichung SI Lab has run 3 rural revitalization cohorts since 2020.

### Problem 4 — Climate change (気候変動 / 氣候變遷)
Adaptation-skewed: heatwaves, typhoon intensity, agricultural disruption, and coastal-city risk. Mitigation welcome but teams must connect to a concrete affected population within their home country, not a global abstraction.

- JP-1 2024 set Japan's hottest-summer record for the 3rd consecutive year.
- JP-2 Agricultural losses from heat-stress at crop level estimated in ¥100B range annually.
- JP-3 Tokyo metro committed to 50% emissions cut by 2030 (Zero Emission Tokyo).
- TW-1 2024 recorded Taiwan's hottest year on record.
- TW-2 Typhoon-related agricultural losses routinely exceed NT$5B in bad years.
- TW-3 Taichung's "2050 Net Zero" city roadmap released 2023.

## 2. Assessment Rubric

Jury per country: **3 people — 1 public-sector expert + 1 private-sector expert + 1 Underdogs coach.** Adopted directly from SU Team's EASI LEAGUE 4-axis design.

| Axis | Weight | What we look for |
|---|---|---|
| **Idea specificity** | 25% | Problem concretely named. Target user specified. Insight is non-obvious. |
| **BM completeness** | 25% | Who pays, how much, through what channel. Unit economics visible. Validation plan attached (raised bar for this track). |
| **Growth potential** | 25% | Credible wedge → expansion path. Scale mechanism named (channel, network, policy). |
| **Impact** | 25% | Split 50/50 between **market size** and **socio-economic ripple** (jobs created, social cost reduced, policy influence). Tied to Outcome, not just Output. |

Scoring: 1–5 per axis per juror. Ties broken by Impact axis score.

## 3. Validation Plan Template (1 page, due end of C2)

Teams fill this in by 12:00 of Day 2.

- **The BM one-liner** (one sentence: for whom, what, how paid).
- **Top 3 assumptions** we must validate in the next 3 months. *(not "is there demand" — specific, falsifiable.)*
- **For each assumption**: validation method (interviews / pilot / landing-page test / partner MoU / paid pre-order / other), sample size or threshold, pass/fail criterion.
- **Partner asks**: what 2 local partners (JP: Musashino / city office / industry assoc; TW: Taichung SI Lab / district office / NGO) would we need, and for what?
- **Cost to validate**: estimated JPY or TWD.
- **Go / no-go checkpoint**: specific date + what decision we'll make then.
- **The "If we fail" note**: what would we learn that is still worth the 3 months?

## 4. Coach Cue Cards — 5 prompts differentiating JP vs TW

Coaches receive a bilingual card set. Cues are same across cohorts in spirit but delivered with different cadence/register.

| # | Moment | JP cue (consensus / preparation culture) | TW cue (speed / showcase culture) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | B3 team formation | *"Before you commit to this team, name the one thing you want to be *carefully* right about by end of Day 2."* Respect careful language; don't rush. | *"Pick a team and go. You can re-pick at dinner if the chemistry is off. Move."* Explicit permission to iterate fast. |
| 2 | B4 first 1:1 (Desire) | *"なぜ他の誰かではなく、あなたがこの問題に取り組むのか?"* — invite slow answer, silence is okay. | *"為什麼是你做這件事?給我一個故事,一分鐘內。"* — push for a story, a minute max. |
| 3 | C1 BM completeness | *"Who is the first paying customer, and what is the exact first conversation you will have with them?"* Encourage detailed, scripted prep. | *"Who pays you NT$1 this week? Not this year — this week. Draft the DM now."* Push to drafted artifact. |
| 4 | C2 Durability | *"5年後、あなたがまだこれをやっていると想像してください。何が変わっていますか?"* Use the future-imagination frame, invite long reflection. | *"5 年後這件事沒做成,最可能的原因是什麼?現在就寫下來。"* Frame as pre-mortem, write immediately. |
| 5 | C3 pitch prep | *"One number, one place, one person. Put those three on slide 1."* Encourage quiet rehearsal before demo. | *"Slide 1 is a photo from the field. Slide 2 is the number. Go practice out loud, now."* Energetic, on-stage rehearsal. |

Coaches should resist applying the other country's cadence onto a cohort. A TW-style "move now" delivered to a JP team can feel abrasive; a JP-style "prepare carefully" delivered to a TW team can feel deflating. Translation of delivered cues beyond what is printed here is `[LOCAL COACH TO FILL IN]`.
