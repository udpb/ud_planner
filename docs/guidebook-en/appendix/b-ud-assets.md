## 2. Proprietary Tools (`UD_PROPRIETARY_TOOLS`)

### ACT-PRENEURSHIP

- **Type**: Diagnostic tool
- **Official notation**: Exact hyphenation and capitalization — `ACT-PRENEURSHIP`
- **Content**: Pre/post measurement of five execution capacities (Goal / Environment / Problem / eXecution / Routinization)
- **Use**: Quantitatively proves participant capacity change; supports pairing and tailored coaching based on pre-diagnostics

### DOGS Team Building

- **Type**: Team-building workshop
- **Official notation**: `DOGS` fixed uppercase
- **Content**: DISC-based 24-item diagnostic plus team-composition solution
- **Use**: Early-session role assignment inside startup teams; pairing aid for matching programs

### 6 Dimension Startup Growth Model

- **Type**: Diagnostic model
- **Official notation**: Keep in English, or use "Startup 6 Dimension"
- **Content**: Comprehensive diagnostic framework across startup-team growth stages (about 60 questions, 6 indicators, hexagonal visualization)
- **Use**: Corporate diagnostic tool for reading each team's needs; rationale for tailored expert matching

### IMPACT Startup Methodology

- **Type**: Education methodology (one of the baseline frameworks for **startup-education-track** curricula)
- **Official notation**: `IMPACT` (uppercase) + "6 stages, 18 modules, 54 questions"
- **Content**:
  - **I** (Ideation): Self — Why do I start a business?
  - **M** (Market): Customer and problem — Who is the first customer and what is the real problem?
  - **P** (Product): Solution — How do we design the core value and solution?
  - **A** (Acquisition): Market entry — How do we acquire the first 50 customers?
  - **C** (Commercial): Commercialization — Is the structure profitable as revenue grows?
  - **T** (Team): Organization and growth — Why do we do this and how do we measure/manage?
- **Applicable scope**: Curricula targeting entrepreneurs and pre-entrepreneurs. Time allocation per stage is adjusted based on project characteristics.
- **Non-applicable projects (caution)**: For projects whose primary purpose is **not** entrepreneur cultivation — e.g., local-commerce revitalization, design/craft competitions, cultural-artisan exchange, employee-matching pro bono, small-business support — apply domain-specific frameworks (local-brand action learning, multi-stage competition design, artisan-exchange design, mentor-matching design, etc.) **instead of IMPACT**. Read this as **"IMPACT for startup education"**, not **"IMPACT for everything."**

### EduBot

- **Type**: AI learning assistant
- **Content**: During the 35-minute workshop, the AI delivers real-time theory explanations, application guidance, feedback, and examples
- **Use**: Describe only as a **tool layer** that strengthens the 4-Tier Support System. Do not represent it as a separate product or a separate tier.

### Underbase LMS

- **Type**: Learning Management System
- **Content**: Proprietary build. Unified management of attendance, assignments, satisfaction surveys, and coaching journals
- **Use**: Builds cross-cohort comparison data; turns accumulated data into an asset

### CORE

- **Type**: Four prerequisite modules
- **Official notation**: `CORE` fixed uppercase
- **Content**: Foundational curriculum that precedes IMPACT

**Notation principle**: All names are drawn from the official `UD_PROPRIETARY_TOOLS` constants. Hardcoding, abbreviation, and casing variation are prohibited.

## 6. Key Message Patterns (`UD_KEY_MESSAGE_PATTERNS`)

Eight patterns to reflect when drafting a proposal:

| Pattern | How to Apply |
|---|---|
| **Domestic first / national first** | Identify a first-in-the-field element (execution-guaranteed · region-anchored · AI-collaboration team) |
| **Quantitative saturation** | Ban `many`, `various`; always give numbers (291 people, 50 companies) |
| **4-Tier Support System** | Not "1 coach" ❌ → expert mentors + consultants + dedicated coach + peer network |
| **Execution guarantee** | "Execution-guaranteed, coaching-centered systematic education" — contrasts theory vs. execution |
| **Proprietary-tool branding** | ACT-PRENEURSHIP · DOGS · 6 Dimension — keep proper names |
| **Section V Bonus Offerings** | 3–4 out-of-RFP-scope proposals (global · impact · investment) |
| **One-Page-One-Thesis** | Each section = one bold headline claim |
| **Government performance readiness** | "Custom performance analysis usable for government performance evaluation" |

## 8. Underdog Redefinition (`UD_UNDERDOG_DEFINITION`)

- **Dictionary meaning**: The weaker side expected to lose in a contest
- **Underdogs redefinition**: A startup is the world's "underdog" — creating something out of a 1% possibility. We believe in the strength of entrepreneurs who challenge the existing system.
- **Conceptual core**: **People who create change through will and influence** — not through social status

**Forbidden**: Using the term inside an "underprivileged / pity" frame.

## 11. LICORN / KOSME LIPS

### LICORN

- **Meaning**: Lifestyle & Local Innovation uniCORN — a unicorn-bound innovation company in the lifestyle and local sectors
- **Role**: The proposer's in-house investment vehicle
- **Section V applications**:
  - Project-centric investment support (up to 10 million KRW per award-winning team)
  - Overseas expo-participation support (approx. 2 million KRW per team in expenses)
  - Participation in the growth-support project community

### KOSME LIPS

- **Meaning**: Ministry of SMEs and Startups LIPS (Small Merchant Matching Loan program for entrepreneurial small-business owners)
- **Role**: Linked to LICORN investment to provide **matching loans up to 5× the investment amount**
- **Use**: Cite in Section V as a pathway to secure additional investment capital and overseas-expansion funding

## 12. Tone Guide (`UD_TONE_GUIDE`)

- **Voice**: Confident declarative — not "we can do X" ❌, but "we do X" ⭕
- **Evidence**: Every claim is backed by quantitative evidence — "together with a coach pool of 291"
- **English mix**: Keep core concepts in English alongside Korean — "Born Global", "Human Touch", "All In One", "MVP Fast Track"
- **Branding**: Enclose core concepts in quotation marks — "4-Tier Pacemaker System"
- **Format**: Use markdown subheadings and lists; target 600–900 Korean characters
- **Structure**: Intro (context, one sentence) → Body (one bold headline + evidence) → Conclusion (promise, one sentence)

## 14. Common Mistakes (Forbidden List, based on `ud-brand-voice` SKILL §11)

1. **Framing "underdog" in a pity register** — violates the Underdog redefinition principle
2. **Presenting "AI Coach" as a standalone product** — only strength-level mentions are allowed
3. **Mixing entity names** (blending "Underdogs", "UD Impact", and "유디임팩트" inside one proposal)
4. **Lowercasing or Koreanizing IMPACT** (e.g., softening it into "임팩트 방법론")
5. **Vague quantity language** ("many", "various", "substantial") — violates the quantitative-saturation principle
6. **Overusing English** — excessive English terminology inside a Korean document
7. **Altering proprietary tool names** (ACT-PRENEURSHIP → "Actpreneurship", DOGS → "Dogs")
8. **Copy-pasting sentences from a winning proposal** — context-free reuse reads awkwardly; recombine and rewrite the source key messages
