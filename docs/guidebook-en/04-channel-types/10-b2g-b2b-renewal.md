# Ch.10 Strategy by Funder Type — B2G · B2B · Renewal

> Reading this chapter, you learn how the tone, structure, and emphasis of a proposal should shift depending on whether the funder is a **government body, a corporation, or a client you already won last year**.

## Why Split by Funder Type

Even under the same banner of a "startup education proposal," the language that lands differs sharply depending on whether the commissioning entity is a **central ministry, a large enterprise, or a client that contracted you the year before**. The evaluator mix is different. Expectations around performance are different. And the list of **phrases to avoid** is different too. Misread the type, blend the tones, and the whole proposal feels off.

This chapter organizes the three types as three cards. Each card contains:

- Key message direction
- Proposal structure characteristics
- Evaluator profile
- Budget tone
- Curriculum and execution design characteristics (theory ratio · Action Week · systems language)
- Cautions
- Links to casebook entries for that type

## Card 1. B2G (Central Ministries and Local Governments)

### Representative Cases

- [NH Agritech](../03-casebook/06-startup-education/nh-agritech.md) — public institution commissioning
- [2025 Seochon, Jongno-gu Local Brand Commercial District Strengthening](../03-casebook/07-local-commerce/jongno-seochon.md) — local government
- [2025 Tourism Competition + Tourism Souvenir Expo](../03-casebook/08-culture-tourism/tourism-souvenir.md) — public institution
- [2025 Hanji Culture Products Design Competition](../03-casebook/08-culture-tourism/hanji-design.md) — Ministry of Culture affiliate
- [2025 Anseong Cultural Town Glocal Specialization Project](../03-casebook/08-culture-tourism/anseong-glocal.md) — local government

### Key Message Direction

- **Declarative voice + policy language + quantitative saturation**
- Phrases that carry weight: "execution-guaranteed," "systematic operation," "performance management," "creation of social value," "regional revitalization," "self-sustainability strengthening"
- Language drawn from government performance evaluation, sustainability, and risk management lands best

### Proposal Structure Characteristics

- **If the RFP specifies a table of contents, never rearrange it**
- Page distribution must mirror the scoring weight table precisely
- The format `I. General Profile → II. Basic Plan → III. Execution Plan → IV. Project Management → V. Additional Offerings` is the safest default
- Section IV (Project Management) cannot be written thin — safety, reporting, risk, and personal data protection carry heavy scoring weight

### Evaluator Profile

- A mix of subject-matter professors and experts, funder-side operational staff, and audit or accounting specialists
- Conservative and risk-averse
- Remembers the failures of prior-year operators — those exact areas need oversaturated reassurance

### Budget Tone

- Budget is not the protagonist — place it toward the end, written plainly
- Emphasize program investment ratio and actual-use ratio ("Program investment 91.2%," "actual-use ratio under 60%")
- A separate page on preventing personnel-cost leakage

### Curriculum and Execution Design Characteristics

- If the theory ratio exceeds 60%, it must be restructured around Action Weeks
- Local institution and resident participation programs must have genuine share (especially for local government projects)
- KPIs must be stated as numbers — satisfaction, completion rate, outcome occurrence rate
- Proprietary tools (ACT-PRENEURSHIP · IMPACT Startup Methodology · DOGS · 6 Dimension Startup Growth Model) are inserted as proof of systematic operation

### Section V Usuals

- Global linkage (Japan and India entities, overseas expos)
- Follow-on investment (LICORN investment, LIPS financing up to 5x)
- Impact reporting (ESG measurement know-how across 1,600 companies, government performance evaluation readiness)
- Post-project community (alumni, peer mentoring)
- Outcome data archiving

### Cautions (Specific to B2G)

1. **Overly innovative or experimental language is a risk.** Phrases like "game changer," "revolutionary," or "disruptive" put public-sector evaluators on edge. Innovation must wear the clothing of **"proven, execution-guaranteed, and systematic."**
2. **Avoid revenue and ROI vocabulary.** B2B terms like "revenue maximization" or "revenue growth rate" do not fit public-sector work. The same substance should be reframed as "economic effect," "regional economic revitalization," or "self-sustainability."
3. **Prepare for government performance evaluation and audits.** Build KPIs and a reporting framework that can stand up to internal audit, administrative audit, and national assembly audit.
4. **Never shortcut personal data protection and safety management.** Required items tucked into the corner of a scoring table — "describe personal data protection measures," "assign a safety management officer" — still carry points.
5. **Pre-arranged awardee possibility is always present.** The more regionally embedded or repeat-awarded the project, the more this needs checking. A phone call with the contact helps read the room.

## Card 2. B2B (Corporations and Foundations)

### Representative Cases

- [Kolon Pro Bono](../03-casebook/06-startup-education/kolon-probono.md) — large enterprise CSR
- [GS Retail Eco Social Impact](../03-casebook/06-startup-education/gs-retail.md) — large enterprise retail B2B, 7 consecutive cohorts

### Key Message Direction

- **Outcome-oriented + ROI language**
- Phrases that carry weight: "speed," "execution capability," "measurable effect," "ROI," "renewal," "scalability"
- Outcome indicators (revenue, conversion rate, number of store placements) are the protagonists

### Proposal Structure Characteristics

- If no table of contents is specified, follow the scoring weight order
- Front sections such as company overview, organization, and track record are **short and dense**
- The core is execution plan, performance indicators, and reporting framework
- Commit to **a performance format that can drop directly into a sustainability report or ESG report**

### Evaluator Profile

- Primarily the funder's strategy, CSR, and ESG staff
- Often includes an external consulting partner
- Business-minded, ROI-focused, decides quickly

### Budget Tone

- Framed as "effect per total spend"
- Describe how the same budget produces a larger effect
- Express costs in a form that allows cost-per-KPI analysis (e.g., investment per completing team, investment per store-placement conversion)

### Curriculum and Execution Design Characteristics

- Minimize theory — center on field practice and real-world testing
- If corporate employees participate, **employee experience design** becomes a second axis
- Embedded measurement tools (ACT-PRENEURSHIP · 6 Dimension Startup Growth Model · DOGS) secure quantitative indicators

### Section V Usuals

- Corporate portfolio expansion (proposals linking to adjacent programs)
- Alumni and re-engagement programs
- Brand-value proof content (employee stories, success-case video, reports formatted for sustainability reporting)
- Global expansion (leveraging Japan and India entities)
- Expansion potential into other departments within the enterprise

### Cautions (Specific to B2B)

1. **No government-work vocabulary.** B2G terms like "execution framework," "project management," or "regional revitalization" feel wrong when mixed in. Rephrase the same substance as "operating framework," "operations management," or "impact creation."
2. **Careful with political language and overloaded social discourse.** ESG is in vogue, but a tone that reads as overtly political or activist makes corporate CSR staff uncomfortable. **"The intersection of business value and social value"** is the safe register.
3. **Always keep renewal in mind.** Even on a first contract, hint at "a structure that can continue next year and the year after." If the proposal reads like a one-off event, renewal will not follow.
4. **Weak measurement and reporting kills renewal.** To reach a 6th or 7th consecutive cohort, KPIs must level up each year, measurement methodology must sharpen, and report formats must evolve. That evolution path has to be visible at the proposal stage.
5. **Respect corporate confidentiality and brand guidelines.** In both the proposal and operations, follow the funder's brand colors, logo usage, and official announcement channel guidelines. External exposure must be handled with care.

## Card 3. Renewal and Continuing Engagements

### Representative Cases

- [GS Retail Eco Social Impact](../03-casebook/06-startup-education/gs-retail.md) — 7 consecutive cohorts
- Other long-running engagements where the renewal register dominates (including multi-phase continuations such as the Seochon local brand three-phase project)

### Key Message Direction

- **Trust-based + improvement-oriented**
- Phrases that carry weight: "N% improvement over last year," "based on cumulative data," "third-year self-sustainability," "the leap to the next stage"
- No first-meeting tone ("a new encounter" or "our first proposal" should not appear)

### Proposal Structure Characteristics

- **Opening page: last year's outcome summary + this year's improvement plan**
- Company introduction can be a single page (the funder already knows us)
- Cumulative data, cohort-by-cohort improvement indicators, and next-stage KPIs go up front
- Clearly distinguish new content from retained strong content

### Evaluator Profile

- Mostly funder-side operational staff (few new evaluators)
- People who have already experienced working with us
- Remembers last year's gaps, feedback, and improvement requests

### Budget Tone

- Explain clearly the rationale for adjustments versus last year's budget
- Logic such as **"this much improvement on the same budget"** or **"an N% increase lets us add this task"**
- Include last year's budget utilization and efficiency analysis

### Curriculum and Execution Design Characteristics

- Separate sessions that are removed, added, or improved versus last year
- A graphic for the cohort evolution path (Cohort 1 → Cohort 2 → ... → current → next)
- Segmentation built on cumulative data (custom tracks by team type)

### Section V Usuals

- Follow-on linkages for top graduating teams from prior cohorts
- Cross-cohort bridging programs (alumni mentoring new cohorts)
- New analyses and reports drawn from cumulative data
- A consolidated case book or white paper across all cohorts

### Cautions (Specific to Renewal)

1. **No "nice to meet you" tone, ever.** Phrases like "let us introduce our firm" should be deleted on sight. The funder already knows us.
2. **You must know last year's gaps.** Phone the contact and confirm **"what was the biggest disappointment last year"** — that becomes the opening section of this year's improvement plan.
3. **"Same KPIs every year" signals lack of growth ambition.** Upgrade each indicator one notch: revenue 200 → 300, completion rate 80% → 90%, intent to re-engage → actual re-engagement rate.
4. **Defensive structure against new entrants.** A renewal engagement invites competitors to try to "flip the table." Lock cumulative data, cohort networks, and learned processes into the proposal as **"assets only we hold."**
5. **Guard against complacency.** "We have run this well for six years, so this year too..." is a warning sign. Approach each draft as if writing from scratch. Evaluators can tell when last year's proposal has been copy-pasted.

| | B2G | B2B | Renewal |
|---|-----|-----|--------|
| **Core language** | Systematic · execution-guaranteed · self-sustainability | Effect · ROI · speed | Cumulative · improvement · next stage |

| **Budget framing** | Actual-use ratio · investment ratio | Cost per effect | Rationale for adjustment vs. last year |
| **Section V** | Global · impact · archiving | Expansion · re-engagement · brand | Alumni · consolidated case book |
| **What to avoid** | Overstated innovation · revenue vocabulary | Government vocabulary · political language | "First time" · copy-paste from last year |

## Handling Borderline Projects

Real RFPs often do not fall cleanly into one of the three types.

- **Corporate CSR with government linkage**: lead with a B2B tone, and add only the performance formats usable for government evaluation. Do not blend the two tones — the strategy is **B2B tone + a quantitatively saturated performance-indicator section.**
- **Local government but globally oriented**: B2G as the base, with a larger share of global linkage inside Section V. The body tone remains "regional revitalization and self-sustainability."
- **First time with a corporation, but pitching a 3-year vision**: lead with a B2B tone for the first proposal, but present a **3-year roadmap** (Year 1 → Year 2 → Year 3) as a dedicated section. Pre-design the renewal.
