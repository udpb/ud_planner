# NH Agritech — B2G Agri-food Startup Education

> Reading this case, you get a feel for how "policy language + quantitative saturation + execution guarantee" combine in a B2G agri-food startup program.

## 1. Business One-Line Summary

- **Funder**: Public institution in agriculture and rural affairs (B2G, policy-implementation consignment)
- **Program Name**: NH Agritech Startup Incubation Program (operated in annual cohorts)
- **Target**: Pre-founders and early-stage founder teams in agritech (agriculture and food technology)
- **Core Tasks**: Founder sourcing → capacity diagnosis → education and coaching → prototype development → retail linkage and investment matching
- **Duration**: Annual cycle (sourcing to completion, approx. 6–9 months)

NH Agritech looks like "an agriculture program, so agricultural expertise must come first," but the actual evaluation point is **"Is there a startup incubation system in place?"** Agricultural domain knowledge is supplied by external mentors; the proposer's weapon must be startup methodology and performance-management capability.

## 2. Key Messages

Summary of the original tone:

> **"농식품 분야 예비창업가의 실행력을 끝까지 책임지는 실행 보장형 애그테크 창업 플랫폼"**
> ("An execution-guaranteed agritech startup platform that takes responsibility, end to end, for the execution capability of agri-food pre-founders.")

Because it is a B2G program, policy language — "execution guarantee," "systematic operation," "performance management" — forms the skeleton of the tone. An excessively innovative or experimental voice burdens evaluators (see Ch.10 B2G cautions).

## 3. Impact Value Chain — 5 Layers

- **Input**: Budget · dedicated PM · agritech mentor pool (network across the Ministry of Agriculture, Food and Rural Affairs, Rural Development Administration, Nonghyup Economic Research Institute, etc.) · proprietary methodologies (ACT-PRENEURSHIP, IMPACT) · proprietary LMS (Underbase)
- **Activity**: Open call for pre-founders → pre-diagnosis (ACT-PRENEURSHIP 5 execution capacities) → IMPACT 6-stage curriculum (Ideation → Market → Product → Acquisition → Commercial → Team) → Action Week inserted (field validation, market-channel testing) → 1:1 coaching + mentor matching → Demo Day
- **Output**: Number of graduating teams (20–30 per cohort), business plans produced, MVP products, pre/post diagnostic data
- **Outcome**: Incorporation rate, follow-on government-program selection rate, revenue-generating team rate, pre/post ACT-PRENEURSHIP score gains
- **Impact**: Expansion of the founder pool within the agritech ecosystem, accumulation of a new-enterprise database in agri-food, benchmarking inquiries from other regions

## 4. Three Differentiation Points

1. **Built-in pre/post diagnostics** — ACT-PRENEURSHIP's 5 execution capacities (Goal / Environment / Problem / eXecution / Routinization) are measured twice, before and after education. Used in the final report as evidence of quantitative outcomes.
2. **IMPACT 6-stage curriculum** — The founder's growth stages are standardized as I → M → P → A → C → T. Each stage has defined core questions and deliverables, giving a consistent roadmap through completion.
3. **4-Tier Support System** — Specialist mentor panel (agri-food domain) + consultant pool (accounting, legal, investment) + dedicated coach (weekly 1:1) + peer network (cohort community). No single-lecturer or single-coach phrasing.

## 5. How Underdogs' Proprietary Tools and Network Worked

- **ACT-PRENEURSHIP**: Diagnostic tool. Cited in the proposal as the basis for "quantitative performance reporting grounded in pre/post diagnostic data."
- **IMPACT Startup Methodology**: Proprietary 6-stage, 18-module, 54-question methodology. The skeleton of curriculum design.
- **DOGS Team Building**: DISC-based 24 questions. Deployed in the first two sessions when role distribution within a founder team is unclear.
- **800-coach pool · 520 global partners**: Basis for quantitative saturation (exact figures in [Appendix B](../../appendix/b-ud-assets.md)).
- **ESG measurement know-how across 1,600 companies**: Applicable to reflecting agri-food ESG issues (carbon, circular economy, fair trade).
- **LICORN investment linkage**: For top-performing graduating teams, a path to proprietary investment or KOSME LIPS financing matching (up to 5×).

## 6. Lessons for New PMs

**(1) B2G tonal instinct.** Policy language such as "systematic operation," "execution guarantee," and "performance management" lands better than "innovation." The agri-food sector is especially conservative, so an overly startup-ish tone backfires. The same content is evaluated differently when dressed as "an execution-guaranteed startup platform" versus "a game-changing agritech innovation."

**(2) Domain expertise is supplied through the external network.** The proposer does not need to be an agricultural specialist. The design hinge is connecting the networks of the Ministry of Agriculture, Food and Rural Affairs, Rural Development Administration, Nonghyup Economic Research Institute, and regional agricultural technology centers as a **specialist mentor panel**. Underdogs is the startup-methodology expert; external mentors are the domain experts — role separation must be clear.

**(3) Quantitative outcome metrics secured via diagnostic tools.** For a B2G program, the final report is weak if it stops at Output-level figures like "X graduates, Y startup teams." Outcome indicators — ACT-PRENEURSHIP pre/post score gains, IMPACT stage-attainment rates — must prove performance, giving an edge for next year's renewal and similar-project contract awards.

**(4) Standard Section V Bonus bundle.** Typical Section V items in programs like this:
- Global linkage (leveraging the Japan and India entities, overseas buyer matching)
- Follow-on investment (LICORN investment screening, LIPS financing matching)
- Post-program community (alumni network, peer mentoring)
- Performance-data archiving (basis for the following year's cohort design)

**(5) Caution.** Startup education programs often produce many graduates but few actual startups. If the funder gives feedback such as "last year had many graduates but few startups," the response must specify a **startup-conversion rate** as a KPI and match it with design (extended Action Week, investors invited to Demo Day, follow-on linkage programs). Without numeric commitments, the same feedback will return the following year.
