# GS Retail Eco Social Impact — B2B Continuous Operation

> Reading this case, you learn how the "accumulated-trust + improvement-oriented" tone works in a program that has run **7 consecutive cohorts** with a B2B corporate partner.

## 1. Business One-Line Summary

- **Funder**: GS Retail (large-enterprise retail, B2B)
- **Program Name**: GS Retail Eco Social Impact Project (2019–2025, 7 consecutive cohorts)
- **Target**: Founder teams in ESG, environment, and social-value domains (small businesses and startups capable of B2B retail touchpoints)
- **Core Tasks**: Sourcing → diagnosis of eco / social-impact capacity → execution-centered coaching → retail-channel (GS25, GS더프레시) shelf-entry linkage → channel expansion
- **Duration**: Annual cycle (cohort-based)

The most important contextual point is that this is a continuous program now in its 7th cohort. A classic success point for a renewal proposal is clearly presenting **"what was lacking last year and how we will fix it this year."** The voice is not that of meeting a new client (building a new relationship) but a **trust-based, improvement-oriented** voice.

## 2. Key Messages

Summary of the original tone:

> **"7년 누적의 데이터로 더 빠르고 더 정확하게 — ESG 유통 접점의 실질적 매출을 만드는 에코 소셜임팩트 플랫폼"**
> ("Faster and more precise, powered by 7 years of accumulated data — an eco social-impact platform that creates real revenue at ESG retail touchpoints.")

The B2B tone means "results-oriented + ROI language" forms the backdrop. Concrete business metrics — revenue, shelf entry, sales volume — take the center. Because this is a renewal, any "nice to meet you" voice is forbidden.

## 3. Impact Value Chain — 5 Layers

- **Input**: Budget · dedicated PM · eco / social mentor panel · retail MD (merchandiser) network (GS Retail internal + partner brands) · proprietary methodologies (IMPACT, ACT-PRENEURSHIP, 6 Dimension Startup Growth Model) · data assets accumulated across cohorts 1–6
- **Activity**: Open call for founder teams (leveraging the 7-year accumulated DB) → 6 Dimension diagnosis (comprehensive assessment by growth stage) → product and branding coaching + retail MD 1:1 consulting → on-site shelf-entry testing (extended Action Week) → pilot entry at GS25 and GS더프레시 → revenue monitoring + improvement cycle
- **Output**: Number of graduating teams (approx. 10–15 per cohort), number of teams placed with retailers, pilot sales volume, number of improved SKUs
- **Outcome**: Average monthly revenue growth rate for participating teams, full-entry conversion rate, contract-extension rate, brand-awareness indicators
- **Impact**: Accumulation of social-impact brands inside GS Retail's ESG portfolio, eco-startup data assets built over 7 years, a peer-growth ecosystem formed through inter-cohort networking

## 4. Three Differentiation Points

1. **Segmentation grounded in 7 years of accumulated data** — Sourcing and coaching data from cohorts 1–6 is accumulated, so the 7th-cohort proposal presents, with data, "which stage of founder team is most likely to reach retail entry." An asset new competitors find hard to follow.
2. **Direct retail MD network** — Beyond the internal GS Retail MD connection, there is an external linkage path to other retail channels (convenience stores, drugstores, lifestyle select shops).
3. **6 Dimension Startup Growth Model applied** — Diagnosing bottlenecks at each growth stage across 6 dimensions. Proves with a tool that teams receiving the same education need different interventions.

## 5. How Underdogs' Proprietary Tools and Network Worked

- **6 Dimension Startup Growth Model**: Growth-diagnostic framework. Each cohort measures participating teams' current stage so that coaching density is differentiated.
- **ACT-PRENEURSHIP**: Quantifies "execution capacity" change through pre/post diagnostics. Especially important KPI evidence for B2B continuous programs.
- **IMPACT Startup Methodology**: The skeleton of educational design. Given the B2B character, time allocation is weighted toward Product, Commercial, and Team stages rather than Ideation and Market.
- **EduBot · Underbase LMS**: Real-time AI support during the 35-minute workshop slot (EduBot); integrated management of attendance, assignments, satisfaction, and coaching logs (LMS). Enables cohort-to-cohort comparative data accumulation.
- **LICORN investment + LIPS financing matching**: Graduating teams whose revenue passes a threshold are linked to proprietary investment or financing (up to 5×).

## 6. Lessons for New PMs

**(1) A renewal proposal is different from a new-client proposal.** In a new-client proposal, company introduction, track record, and credibility take up the first third; in a renewal proposal, a single page is enough for that. Instead, **"last year's performance summary + this year's improvements"** must come first. The funder already knows us.

**(2) Quantitative targets are upgraded every year.** If cohort 6's KPI was "monthly revenue ₩2M achieved," cohort 7 should present a more demanding figure such as "monthly revenue ₩3M + 70% re-entry rate." Corporate partners read a repeated, unchanged KPI as lack of ambition.

**(3) The power of data as an asset.** Expose the accumulated data (cohorts 1–6) in the proposal as "an asset only we possess." Even if new competitors propose on the same theme, one line — "segmentation grounded in 7 years of data" — widens the evaluation gap. **Data is not built overnight**; the craft is making evaluators feel this viscerally.

**(4) Retail-channel linkage spelled out concretely.** Do not write "retail channel linkage"; write "pilot testing at 2,000 GS25 stores → the top 20% SKUs by response advance to full entry." In B2B proposals, abstract expressions signal the absence of ROI.

**(5) Caution — tone migration.** Even at a B2B funder like GS Retail, while "social value · sustainability · ESG" is the core vocabulary, mixing in **government-operations vocabulary** (implementation framework, social-value creation, use in government-performance evaluation) feels off. B2G and B2B tones must be kept separate. For boundary programs (CSR but with government linkage), the right approach is not to blend the two tones but **B2B tone + quantitative saturation on performance indicators**.
