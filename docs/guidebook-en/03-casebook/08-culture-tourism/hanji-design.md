# 2025 Hanji Culture Product Design Competition — "Hanji Re:Craft"

> Read this case to learn how to combine a **competition judging design (with a 10-point public jury panel weighting) + a 4-stage post-award support system (Startup 6 Dimension diagnosis → matched coach → Action Club → LICORN investment)**.

## 1. Project in One Line

- **Project**: 2025 Hanji Culture Product Design Competition operations contract
- **Funder**: Korea Craft & Design Foundation (KCDF) (B2G · under the Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism)
- **Budget**: ₩100M (incl. VAT)
- **Duration**: contract date ~ December 15, 2025
- **Target**: individuals, groups (up to 3 people), and companies interested in designing/developing culture products using Hanji (Korean mulberry paper)
- **Core tasks**:
  1. Competition operations support (competition operation · 2 consulting rounds · award ceremony)
  2. Sales and distribution follow-up support (participation in Craft Trend Fair · exhibition curation at Hanji Gaheon · listings on 3+ retail channels)
  3. Integrated PR strategy and execution

This is the 3rd-year phase of a program: 2023 (experimentation) → 2024 (value-setting) → **2025 (expansion of possibility)**. The core task: **ensure the winning entries actually succeed in the market, not just end with one-time discovery.**

## 4. Three Differentiators

### (1) A 30-Person Public Jury Panel — 10 Quantitative Points in the 3rd Final Round

3rd final round scoring (105 total, including 5 bonus points):
- Creativity and concept clarity: 25 points
- Feasibility: 20 points
- Design / physical completeness: 20 points
- Presentation clarity / persuasiveness: 15 points
- **Public jury panel: 10 points** ← the core differentiator
- Bonus (overseas sales channel / global-market fit): 5 points

**Public jury panel operations**:
- Approx. 30 people recruited via open application or nomination
- Invited on-site to the 3rd PT round to view the exhibition and the full presentations
- Individual evaluation sheets (online) after each entry's presentation
- Average score quantified into a 10-point weight within the 3rd round

**Why this is powerful**: formally reflecting **actual market response** inside an "expert-centered judging" model creates a mechanism that selects **entries with market viability**, not just artistic merit. It closes the funder's biggest worry — "the winners don't sell" — at the judging stage itself.

### (2) 4-Stage Post-Award Support — Startup 6 Dimension + Matched Coach + Action Club + LICORN Investment

**Stage-by-stage post-award support design**:

1. **Startup 6 Dimension enterprise-diagnosis tool**: 60 or so questions produce 6 indicators → a hexagonal visualization of the company's current state, strengths, and weaknesses. Team self-diagnosis + analysis of submitted materials.
2. **Matched coach assignment**: not just an expert, but a **coach dedicated to nurturing creators**. Beyond design completeness, stretches to commercialization execution.
3. **Action Club (linked to the proposing firm's growth-support program)**: winners join the proposing firm's internal incubation community. 20+ startup programs operated each year.
4. **LICORN investment**: product-centered investment backing global-expansion viability. LICORN = Lifestyle & Local Innovation uniCORN. Up to 5× loan matching via the MSS LIPS linkage.

**From the funder's view**: the biggest differentiator is that the proposal offers a **full-lifecycle incubation path** — "discovery → commercialization → distribution → investment" — rather than a competition that merely "discovers." It directly answers the limitation of prior cohorts (2023 / 2024) — insufficient linkage between distribution, sales, and branding — with concrete remedies.

### (3) Staged Consulting + Diagnostic Tools + Expert Pool

**Consulting design specificity**:
- For the 1st-round 30 teams: branding / design enhancement + IP-protection basics
- For the 2nd-round 10 teams: domestic / overseas distribution and sales-channel entry strategy + business model / revenue structure design
- **Hexagonal enterprise-diagnosis tool** to identify each team's needs, followed by 1:1 expert matching

**Expert pool (by original titles)**:
- Hanji industry specialists (artists / company heads)
- Craft / art-based select-shop operators
- Experts in IP / product design / patents / art goods (including the creator of a character IP with ₩20B+ in 2024 related revenue)
- Global marketing / distribution experts (fairs, crowdfunding, live commerce)
- Overseas sales-channel pioneers and craft-sector experts (past winners of 2010 Asia Design Award etc.)

## 6. What a New PM Should Learn From This Case

**(1) Competition design must run the full cycle: "discover → incubate → distribute → invest."** This is a ₩100M competition project, but winning is not the end — **4 stages of post-award management** follow. If a competition proposal stops at "winner list → award ceremony," that is the 2024-and-earlier tone. If it flows into "winner → distribution → sales → investment," that is the 2025-and-later tone. Funders are sensitive to this difference.

**(2) The meaning of the 10-point public jury panel**. When you let the public into the judging design, **"market-fit validation is built into the competition itself."** It pre-answers the classic problem — "experts pick winners that don't sell" — at the judging stage. The pattern of formally reflecting public participation as a weighted share **when you design the scoring rubric** is portable to many other competitions.

**(3) Task density against budget**. A ₩100M budget packs in competition + 60 consulting sessions + award ceremony + Craft Trend Fair + Hanji Gaheon + 3 retail-platform listings per team + integrated PR. How is this possible?
- Venues use the funder's existing assets (DDP Design Lab, Craft Trend Fair)
- The expert pool is the proposing firm's own network
- Retail platform listings come through the proposing firm's existing partnerships
- Global expansion is Section V — the proposing firm's own project (a Maison&Objet participation slot)

**"Many tasks against a small budget" only becomes feasible through asset reuse.** A new PM faced with a ₩100M RFP will initially feel "this budget can't cover everything," but combining the proposing firm's existing assets with the funder's existing assets makes it possible.

**(4) The power of embedded diagnostic tools**. The "hexagonal enterprise-diagnosis tool" appears across multiple parts of the proposal:
- Pre-step for consulting matching
- Basis for tailored expert connection
- Stage 1 of post-award support
- A quantitative metric for outcomes

**Reusing the same tool across multiple sections** raises the proposal's coherence and strengthens the "systematic operation" impression. A proposal built only from external vendors, with no proprietary tool, cannot match this density.

**(5) A staged view of global expansion**. The proposal lays out a **5-stage roadmap**: 2023 (experimentation) → 2024 (value-setting) → 2025 (expansion of possibility) → 2026 (global expansion) → 2027 and beyond (global trend). Even for a single-year project, a 5-year vision signals to the funder that "this firm has long-term perspective." For a 3rd-year renewal project especially, this roadmap is essential.

**(6) Retail-platform classification at a detailed level**. The proposal classifies retail platforms into 5 types:
- Open market (Naver, 29cm, idus)
- Funding-centered (Wadiz, Tumblbug)
- Lifestyle trend (Ohouse, SSG, Lotte ON)
- Craft-specialized (ChapterOne, Object, Olive Young N)
- Premium retail (Point of View, National Museum of Korea)

For each platform type, the **suitable team / product conditions** and **support structure** are spelled out separately. Not a generic phrase like "retail support" — this level of classification is what builds trust that "they can actually support this."

**(7) Caution — validating that "winners actually sell."** The 4-stage post-award support sounds great, but in reality all 4 winning teams will not reach the LICORN investment stage. Usually 1–2. The 4-stage design in the proposal means **"we keep the full-cycle path open,"** not "every winning team will receive investment." If you fail to manage this distinction after the win, the funder may push back with "this is different from what was promised."
