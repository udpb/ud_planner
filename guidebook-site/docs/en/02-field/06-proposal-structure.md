# Ch.6 Proposal Slide Structure

> Reading this chapter, you gain criteria for deciding, after receiving an RFP, "what sections, how many pages each, and in what tone" to structure your slides.

## 1. Standard Section Structure (I–V)

Underdogs' baseline proposal skeleton is **5 sections**. If the RFP specifies a table of contents, follow it exactly. Otherwise, use this as the default structure.

| Section | Title | Core | Share of Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| **I** | General Profile | Company introduction · Organization · Track record · Financials | 10–15% |
| **II** | Basic Plan | Proposal background · Strategy · Staffing · Roadmap · Expected outcomes | 20–25% |
| **III** | Execution Plan | **The heart of the proposal** — curriculum · coaches · impact · operational detail | 40–50% |
| **IV** | Project Management | Quality assurance · reporting · budget | 10–15% |
| **V** | Additional Offerings | 3–4 bonuses outside the RFP scope — **key differentiation point** | 5–10% |

### Why Section III is half

Evaluators decide "can they really do this?" here. Specific schedules, session-level content, coach profiles, Action Week design, and measurement plans go here. **The highest-weighted scoring item usually maps to this section.**

### Why Section V must not be dropped

A place to propose "we can also do this" beyond what the RFP asks. This is where competitors differ most. Examples:

- "Global-linkage program (Tokyo-entity leverage)"
- "Post-project impact report (SROI measurement)"
- "Alumni network connection"
- "Self-investment firm LICORN as a linkage path"

## 2. One-Page-One-Thesis Principle

**One page = one claim.**

```
[Color header bar] → section marker
[Subheading] → context-setting
[ONE BOLD declarative headline] → the one thing this page says
[Structured visual material] → evidence backing the headline
```

### Why this approach

Evaluators **do not read proposals from start to finish.** They flip pages, scan headlines, and read closely only where interest arises. Without a headline or with multiple topics on one page, the impression is "disorganized proposal."

### Good vs. bad headlines

| Bad | Issue | Good |
|---|---|---|
| "Curriculum composition" | No claim | "Execution-centered 12-week curriculum — 3 Action Weeks yielding 90% MVP production rate" |
| "Organizational composition" | List-form title | "Dedicated PM placement + 4-tier support enables simultaneous operation for 1,500 participants" |
| "Budget plan" | A budget table alone is not enough | "Program investment ratio 91.2% — a budget structure focused on outcomes" |

## 3. Quantitative Saturation Principle

**Vague expressions like "many," "various," "considerable" are forbidden.**

```
❌ "We have many coaches."
⭕ "We have a pool of 800 expert coaches nationwide."

❌ "We have abundant experience."
⭕ "Over 10 years, 498 programs, 20,211 entrepreneurs trained."

❌ "We collaborate with various partners."
⭕ "520+ global partners, simultaneous operations across 96 regions."
```

Distribute **5–10 numbers across the full proposal**. If they are concentrated in one section, the rest looks thin.

## 4. Organizational Structure Representation

**Repeatedly emphasize "dedicated" roles.** Evaluators check whether the team is dedicated to this project or shared across projects.

```
CEO (overall responsibility)
└ Project PM (division-head level, dedicated)
  └ Functional leads (planning / operations / training content / PR)
    └ CM (Coach Manager) — overall coach management
      └ Nationwide coaches + regional PMs
```

Where possible, use real names and photos. "Hong Gil-dong, Division Head (dedicated)" is clearly stronger than "to be assigned."

## 5. Support Structure Representation

Representing the people and organizations supporting the beneficiary with a **single label** (e.g., "1 assigned coach") makes the support structure read as thin. Depending on the business type, it is effective to diagram it as an appropriate **layered structure**.

### Underdogs' 4-Tier Support System (typically applied in startup and small-business training)

1. **Expert mentor pool** — 300+ domain experts, quarterly expert advice
2. **Consultant pool** — 1:1 consulting in deep areas (accounting, legal, investment, global)
3. **Dedicated coach (action coach)** — weekly 1:1 coaching, execution traction, progress checks
4. **Peer network** — cohort learning, alumni community, collaboration linkage

### Support structure choice by business type

| Business Type | Appropriate Support Structure |
|---|---|
| Startup / small-business training | 4-Tier Support System (default) |
| Competitions / design | Jury panel + domain consultants + retail MD |
| Culture / artisan exchange | Domestic artisan network + overseas exchange partners + dedicated operations secretariat |
| Matching (mentoring / pro bono) | Employee / mentor pool + beneficiary-org liaison + matching operator |
| Local commerce | Commercial-district strengthening body + residents' council + external task force + operations secretariat |
| Global expansion | Domestic mentors + local partners + buyer network + overseas entity |

**Principle**: Compose **at least 3 layers** fitting the business type. Avoid single-operator expressions.

## 6. Budget Representation

The budget is not the protagonist of the proposal. Represent it **plainly at the end**.

Core messages:
- Frame **efficiency** as in "Program investment ratio 91.2%"
- Show a high direct-cost ratio (≥ 70% for B2G is recommended)
- Keep margin conservative (10–15%); excess triggers point deductions

**Absolutely do not**: Fix the budget first and trim the curriculum to match it. If the highest-weighted scoring item is "Curriculum, 30 points" and you cut sessions to fit the budget, you throw those 30 points away.

## 7. Drafting Order (before slide writing)

This mirrors Pitfall #1 in Ch.5:

```
1. Impact Value Chain first (Ch.4)
2. Scoring-weight analysis (Ch.3 First 24 Hours)
3. Confirm 3 key messages
4. Table of contents + page allocation
5. Only then, slide writing
```

**Lesson from Kolon Pro Bono**: Writing slides without a Value Chain caused 2 weeks of infinite revision. After establishing the Value Chain, the proposal was completed in 1.5 days.

- [ ] Are vague quantity expressions ("many," "various") at **zero**?
