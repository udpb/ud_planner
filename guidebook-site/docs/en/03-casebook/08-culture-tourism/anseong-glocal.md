# 2025 Anseong Cultural Town Glocal Program — "Weaving Heritage, Sharing Culture"

> Read this case to learn how to organize the Value Chain of a complex program — one that uses **global infrastructure (Tokyo office · Asia Tomorrow · Maison&Objet)** — into 5 clean layers. Of all the cases in this book, Anseong has the most complex Value Chain, which makes it an ideal study for new PMs learning **how to organize a complicated project**.

## 1. Project in One Line

- **Project**: 2025 Anseong Cultural Town Glocal Program operations contract
- **Funder**: Anseong City (B2G · local government)
- **Budget**: ₩650M (incl. VAT)
- **Duration**: contract date ~ December 5, 2024 / approx. 5 months
- **Target**: Anseong cultural artisans (up to 20) + domestic and overseas cultural artisans + visitors
- **Core tasks**:
  1. Glocal insight exchange for cultural artisans (visits to overseas craft-specialized cities)
  2. Domestic (cross-regional) cultural-artisan exchange (networking and co-creation with artisans from other regions)
  3. Glocal artisan invitation-exchange (inviting renowned overseas artisans for collaborative design)
  4. Anseong Cultural Town Glocal Festival (late October)
  5. PR materials production and PR marketing

Anseong holds traditional artisan heritage — 안성맞춤 (Anseong-matchum, "perfect fit"), 유기 (yugi, brassware), and 입사 (ipsa, silver inlay) — and is the only city in the capital region designated as a Republic of Korea Cultural City (approx. ₩20B across 3 years of national and local government funding). With a **Korea-China (Huzhou)-Japan (Kamakura) tri-national exchange network** already in place, 2025 is the year Anseong leaps into a glocal circulation platform.

## 2. Key Message

Original:

> **"Weaving Heritage, Sharing Culture — 유산을 엮어 세계로 나누다"**
> (*Weaving heritage, sharing culture — weaving heritage and sharing it with the world*)
> **"Weave the Heritage"**

Composition:
- **Weaving** (to weave, to interlace — a craft metaphor): as a hub for global artisan cultural exchange, weaving artisans' cultures and values together — starting with East Asian artisan cultural exchange, growing into a global artisan circulation platform
- **Heritage**: the temporal value and cultural heritage of Anseong's artisans, connected globally, forming an image of a cultural city where tradition and heritage are alive and breathing
- **Sharing Culture**: a declaration that Anseong is not just preserving, but becoming **a creative connection point that weaves heritage together with artisans across the world**

"Craft and tradition" · "connection and inheritance" · "local and global" — a glocal circulation platform that stitches all three together. Local-government tone, yet carrying a global-expansion vision — a balance.

## 3. Impact Value Chain — 5 Layers

The Anseong case has many layers and is genuinely complex. Remember this — **the more complex the project, the more critical it is to separate the 5 layers cleanly, because that is what decides the proposal's persuasiveness.**

- **Input**:
  - Budget ₩650M
  - Anchor-program linkage (Republic of Korea Cultural City Anseong — ₩20B across 3 years in national + local funding, East Asia Cultural City)
  - Dedicated PM (9+ years in culture and arts, group-lead level)
  - 9-person staffing (head of operations · team lead · managers, across the MICE group + PR group + tradition/content incubation group)
  - Cultural Artisan Special Advisory Committee (up to 10, domestic and overseas craft experts)
  - **Tokyo office** (established February 2025)
  - Asia Tomorrow platform
  - Maison&Objet participation confirmed
  - Existing tri-national exchange network (Anseong–Huzhou–Kamakura)
- **Activity**:
  - **1. Glocal insight exchange (overseas)**: select 1 city among Kyoto (Japan), Jingdezhen (China), and Tainan (Taiwan) → visit craft-specialized institutions, universities, and residencies → exchange workshops at artisan studios → museum / expo visits with docent tours (3 nights 4 days, August)
  - **2. Domestic cultural-artisan exchange**: exchange with cultural events and residencies in other regions → craft / design exchange and visits to Anseong → linked into the Glocal Festival
  - **3. Glocal artisan invitation-exchange**: invite 7 renowned overseas artisans → 2 Anseong visits → co-design **7+ works** with Anseong cultural artisans
  - **4. Anseong Cultural Town Glocal Festival (late October)**: turn the entire Anseong Cultural Town special street into a cultural festival venue → experience / market / curated exhibitions / networking / environment-building programs
  - **5. Integrated PR**: domestic and overseas PR marketing (target 20M viral views)
  - **6. Special Advisory Committee operations**: Aug–Nov, biweekly meetings, program advisory, network securing
- **Output**:
  - 1 overseas visit (2 on-site meetings, 2+ participating cities, 2+ exchange activities)
  - 30 cultural-artisan participants selected
  - 2+ exchanges via other-region events, 2+ linked collaborative events
  - 7 renowned overseas artisans invited
  - 7+ collaborative designs produced
  - 5 festival programs (experience, market, 2 curated exhibitions, networking, environment-building)
  - 20+ market teams using vacant storefronts along the special street
  - 1 insight-exchange video
  - 5+ meetings with related agencies and experts
- **Outcome**:
  - Participant satisfaction 9+ out of 10
  - 500,000 cumulative visitors across Anseong's combined festival calendar
  - 90%+ participant satisfaction with festival / program
  - 20M viral views from domestic and overseas PR
  - Overseas network secured for domestic cultural artisans · launch pad for global expansion
  - Accumulated co-creation experience among Anseong cultural artisans
- **Impact**:
  - **Anseong Cultural Town cemented as the core content of "the city of artisan and craft culture distribution"**
  - Branded as a hub for global artisan cultural exchange
  - Tangible outcomes from the Korea-China-Japan Asian Cultural City alliance
  - Global distribution network for Anseong cultural artisans (10+ listings on overseas select shops / distributors)
  - Synergy with anchor programs (Cultural City Anseong · East Asia Cultural City)
  - A foundation for traditional artisans' temporal heritage to expand into **everyday luxury brands in global lifestyle**

## 4. Three Differentiators

### (1) Layered Linkage with Anchor Programs — a Glocal Artisan Cultural Circulation Platform

Looked at alone, the Anseong Cultural Town Glocal program is a small project. But the differentiator in this proposal is **how it is designed to layer with anchor programs**:

- **Anchor 01**: Anseong-matchum Cultural Artisan School (artisan incubation)
- **Anchor 02**: Anseong-matchum Cultural Creation Platform (creation space)
- **Anchor 03**: Anseong Artisan Residency
- **Anchor 04**: 6070 Memory Street
- **Festival**: Anseong Baudeokyi Festival (based on Baudeokyi, a Joseon-era "idol" figure)

The declaration is that these anchors are woven into a circulation structure — "**artisan incubation → glocal exchange → global festival**." A single-year ₩650M project is positioned as the core content of the ₩20B, 3-year Cultural City program.

### (2) Tokyo Office + Asia Tomorrow + Maison&Objet — Triple Global Infrastructure

"Global expansion support" is not a slogan but **real infrastructure**:

- **Tokyo office (established February 2025)**: on-the-ground response for Kyoto / Osaka · real-time casting · on-site operations
- **Asia Tomorrow platform**: global content platform (internally launched, 2024)
- **Maison&Objet participation confirmed**: Autumn 2025 Maison&Objet (France) · 4+ Anseong cultural artisans supported to participate
- **Taiwan / China partner firms**
- **European expansion support partner**

Even the craft-specialized cities chosen for the overseas visit are drawn from the **cities where the proposing firm already has a partner pool** (Kyoto MOCAD · Kyoto Traditional Industries Promotion Center · Kyoto University of the Arts · Jingdezhen Ceramic University · Tainan Creative Expo Taiwan). Marking this as "pool-owned" makes it explicit to evaluators.

### (3) Structure That Bundles Complex Tasks Under a Single Key Message

As said earlier, this program has 5 tasks, each containing several sub-programs. The structure could easily slip into "we do a little of everything." Here is how the proposal tamed the complexity:

- The **"Weaving Heritage" umbrella message** ties all tasks together
- Each task is classified under 4 keyword frames — **Heritage · Weaving · Contents · Global**
- Quantitative KPIs (30 participants · 7 collaborative designs · 500,000 festival visitors · 20M viral views · 10 global distribution placements) quantify each task's performance
- A **KEY VALUE & ACTION table** pairs value and action

## 5. How Underdogs' Own Tools and Networks Show Up

- **Tokyo office (established February 2025)**: the core infrastructure for global-network linkage. Front line for Kyoto response in Japan.
- **Asia Tomorrow platform**: a global content platform featuring domestic and overseas on-the-ground startup cases. Internally launched in 2024.
- **Maison&Objet participation contract**: Autumn 2025 Maison&Objet (France) participation confirmed.
- **Data from incubating 20,000 practitioners + 261 action coaches + 400 regional experts** (original figures): the quantitative grounding for the "talent-centered exchange / incubation expert firm" positioning.
- **Master's-level expert pool (craft / history / city / tourism)**: 6+ years project experience. Operational track record — Joseon Royal Tombs (UNESCO World Heritage), Culture Station Seoul 284, etc.
- **LICORN investment + LIPS loan matching**: Section V Bonus. Overseas expo participation support for 4+ teams, up to 5× loan matching on invested capital.
- **Global retail platform network**: the global version of domestic platforms like Wadiz / idus, plus overseas select-shop / distributor listing linkages.

## 6. What a New PM Should Learn From This Case

**(1) How to organize a complex project**. This program has 5 tasks · dozens of sub-programs · 4–5 participant groups (Anseong artisans · domestic artisans · overseas artisans · visitors · operating partners) · multiple venues (Anseong special street · Kyoto · Maison&Objet on-site). Before writing a draft, you **must** do the following:

- A one-page Value Chain 5-layer summary
- Task-level keyword frames (Heritage · Weaving · Contents · Global)
- Quantitative KPI table (task × figure)
- Timeline chart (August overseas insight → October festival → November advisory committee · December report)
- Staffing matrix (9 people × role × task)

Build these 5 single-page pieces first, then write the sections. The more complex the project, the more this one-pager is your lifeline.

**(2) The power of anchor-program linkage**. Tying a single-year ₩650M project to a ₩20B, 3-year Cultural City program positions it as **"the ₩650M that is the core content of the ₩20B."** When a new PM writes a proposal for a local-government project, placing **"how our project contributes to which upper-level policy / anchor program of this local government"** on the front page changes the evaluator's view.

**(3) Quantified notation for global infrastructure**. Not "we have an overseas network," but:
- Tokyo office (established February 2025, real-time response)
- Asia Tomorrow platform (internally launched)
- Maison&Objet Autumn participation confirmed (2024 Spring / Autumn — each with 100+ consultations · ₩100M in export contracts)
- Partner pool explicitly named (Kyoto MOCAD · Kyoto University of the Arts · Jingdezhen Ceramic University etc. — **specific names**)
- Taiwan / China partner firms · European expansion support partner

**"Owned" is the word, but who you own up to — with specific names — is what creates persuasiveness.** Abstract phrasing breeds distrust.

**(4) Design of the Special Advisory Committee**. A committee of up to 10 is designed not as bare "advisors" but across **4 roles**:
- Program planning / operations advisory
- Network securing (linkages among domestic craft artists / cultural institutions · connections to overseas craft institutions / artists)
- Reviewing approaches to co-creation and joint exhibitions
- Project PR

A committee cannot be drawn as a pure cost center — it must be drawn as an **actually working engine**. This proposal operationalizes it in detail: biweekly meetings from August to November, with the operations secretariat handling meeting preparation, minutes, advisory fee disbursement, and reflection of meeting outcomes into operations.

**(5) The instinct for choosing an overseas destination**. Select 1 city among Kyoto (Japan), Jingdezhen (China), and Tainan (Taiwan). Each city's differentiator is laid out in a table:

| Country | City | Insight point |
|---|---|---|
| Japan | Kyoto | Modern inheritance of traditional techniques / sustainability components, craft + design + distribution combined as a business model |
| Taiwan | Tainan | Handmade / museum / experience venue / shop forming one circulation structure, craft-artist-centered platform |
| China | Jingdezhen | Artisan-centered residencies, a regional-regeneration craft culture district strategy |

Final selection is made after consultation with Anseong City — leaving the decision authority with the funder. That kind of flexibility scores points in local-government programs.

**(6) Expansion into a global festival**. Linked with the Anseong Baudeokyi Festival (the city's existing festival built around Baudeokyi), the proposal targets a cumulative **500,000 visitors across Anseong's combined festival calendar**. Presenting a **cumulative KPI including adjacent programs**, not just a single-project KPI, is an effective pattern in local-government proposals.

**(7) Caution — the reality of global collaboration**. "7 renowned overseas artisans invited · 7 collaborative designs" are written as hard numbers, but in real operations:
- Casting renowned overseas artisans takes a long time (minimum 3–6 months of advance coordination)
- Collaborative designs require mandatory copyright / IP review
- Invitation costs (airfare, lodging, per-diem) must sit accurately in the budget
- Visit schedules must sync with the festival schedule (time zone, visa, cultural differences)

It is safer to get **pre-win** provisional confirmation from overseas partners about actual feasibility. Without a partner pool, these number-KPIs are extremely risky commitments.

**(8) Practice problem — clarifying the 5 layers**. Rewrite this program yourself in the 5 layers. At first the boundary between Activity and Output will feel blurry. "Running an artisan-studio exchange workshop" is Activity; "30 participating artisans · 1 exchange-content piece" is Output. "Anseong artisans gaining an overseas network / accumulated co-creation experience" is Outcome. "Anseong positioned as the hub for glocal artisan cultural exchange" is Impact. Anseong is the best case to practice these distinctions on.
