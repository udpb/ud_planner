# 2025 Tourism Contest + Tourism Souvenir Expo — "Streaming K-Souvenir"

> Read this case to learn how to bundle a **composite mandate (competition + expo + distribution + global)** under a single key message, and how to position quantitative targets (₩600M in participant sales, ₩100M in exports, 200 buyer consultations).

## 1. Project in One Line

- **Project**: 2025 Korea Tourism Contest (Souvenir Category) and Korea Tourism Souvenir Expo operations contract
- **Funder**: Korea Tourism Organization affiliate (B2G · policy execution type)
- **Budget**: approx. ₩1.32B (incl. VAT) — the largest scale in this casebook
- **Duration**: contract date ~ approx. 9 months
- **Target**: tourism souvenir development / manufacturing / distribution companies (mixed pool of local players, small businesses, small brands, and large brands)
- **Core tasks**:
  1. Overall planning and shared operations for the tourism souvenir program
  2. Formation and operation of a tourism souvenir growth council
  3. Planning and operation of the Korea Tourism Contest (Souvenir Category)
  4. Distribution and sales-channel support for winning entries
  5. Planning and operation of the Korea Tourism Souvenir Expo

Since 1998, each region of Korea has discovered tourism souvenirs through this program. In 2025 it was redesigned along a new axis — **"domestic sales → overseas export"** — with "Beyond K" as the key message, putting real weight on global expansion outcomes.

## 2. Key Message

Original:

> **"Streaming K-Souvenir — 세계인의 관광 플레이리스트에 K-Souvenir을 스트리밍하다"**
> (*Streaming K-Souvenir into the world's tourism playlist*)

"Stream" (a current, a flow, something that keeps flowing) is used with layered meaning:
- **K-Streaming**: connecting K-content's streaming consumption culture with tourism souvenirs
- **Flexible + Connected**: a flow that bends smoothly into the visit patterns of global tourists
- **Beyond K**: an era in which Korean value leads the globe — a New K (NEW K / New Hallyu)

The 2024 key message, "A souvenir village where the festival opens — local and tourism," was shifted in 2025 toward a **global-expansion-centric strategy**. The tone carries a brand pivot to a mega-hit expo and an identity change into a global-festival-type expo.

## 4. Three Differentiators

### (1) Four Strategies (multi-price-tier competition + domestic/overseas distribution collaboration + mega-hit expo + global buyer linkage)

Based on Good / Develop Point analysis against the prior year, four strategies were proposed:

- **Strategy 01**: Multi-price-tier Korea–Local–Global-linked competition (general, premium, and an under-₩30K tier to secure mass-market appeal)
- **Strategy 02**: Expanded domestic/overseas distribution collaboration projects (centered on curated fairs and promotions)
- **Strategy 03**: Buzz-building for mega-hit expo growth (influencers, celebrities, mega-brand participation)
- **Strategy 04**: Global buyer-linked expansion support (pre-consulting + invited consultation sessions + Maison&Objet participation)

Each strategy carries **concrete execution methods + quantitative targets**, proving these are not "strategies made only of words."

### (2) "Mega-Hit Expo" Track Record

Numbers cited include paid visitors of 10,000+ · daily sales of ₩300M+ · 10 mega-brand participants · a DDP full-venue event with 100,000 visitors. Not just "expo operation experience" but a quantified **"experience turning expos into mega hits."**

Strategy 03's buzz-building plan includes:
- Brand pivot to a local + global festival
- Influencer and celebrity casting targeted at the three segments — domestic mass audience, foreign tourists, and international students
- Track records cited: 2023 Pokémon pop-up run twice consecutively (5-hour wait, ₩100M daily sales), 2023–24 Game Culture Festival with 130,000 visitors

### (3) **Real** Paths for Global Expansion

"Global expansion support" here is not a slogan but a real path:
- Japan subsidiary (established 2025) — used for Osaka Expo / Tokyo on-site pop-ups
- Maison&Objet Autumn 2025 participation contract already signed (the proposing firm's own global expansion project)
- 2024 Spring Maison&Objet (Gwangju local brand participation) — **100+ buyer consultations · ₩100M in export contracts achieved**
- 2024 Autumn Maison&Objet (Korea Ceramic Foundation) — **100+ buyer consultations · ₩100M in export contracts achieved**
- Specialized partner firm for domestic/overseas buyer linkage (cumulative transaction value ₩3B+ · 488+ export deals)

## 6. What a New PM Should Learn From This Case

**(1) Density of the key message**. "Streaming K-Souvenir" packs **six contexts into a single phrase**:
- K-content's streaming consumption culture
- The image of tourism souvenirs flowing into everyday global life
- A declaration of the Beyond K (New Hallyu) era
- A globalized evolution of the earlier "souvenir village where the festival opens" message
- A pairing of the keywords "flexible" and "connected"
- A naming that centers on real sales and export results

For a proposal's key message to hold **this many contexts at once**, you have to spend real time finding the intersection between the RFP's body keywords (K-souvenir, global expansion, New Hallyu) and the proposing firm's own slogans. You cannot just staple on "nice-sounding words" in a hurry.

**(2) A single umbrella for composite mandates**. Competition + consulting + expo + distribution + global — each of the five tasks is large. But under the "Streaming K-Souvenir" umbrella, each task is positioned as **"one segment of the path flowing out to the global market."** When the mandate is multifaceted, **you need an umbrella that bundles everything under one message** or evaluators cannot hold it in mind. Without that, each task drifts apart and the proposal reads as "we do a little of everything."

**(3) Concrete quantitative targets + evidence from past results**. The numbers are all there — ₩600M in sales, ₩100M in exports, 200 buyer consultations, 10,000 visitors, 130 exhibiting companies. What keeps them from floating in mid-air is that they are **backed by prior results**:
- 2024 Maison&Objet — 100+ buyer consultations · ₩100M in export contracts (twice in a row)
- 2023 Pokémon pop-up — 5-hour wait, ₩100M daily sales
- 2023 Game Culture Festival — 130,000 visitors
- DDP Fashion & Beauty Expo — 10,000 paid visitors, ₩300M daily sales

The pattern is **"target number = past result × improvement rate"** as justification. When a new PM writes quantitative targets without a foundation like this, the numbers ring hollow.

**(4) Global linkage must be a real route**. Abstract phrasing like "overseas expansion support" carries no value. Instead: **"Maison&Objet Autumn participation confirmed, 2+ teams supported"** — with a specific expo name, specific support scale, and specific schedule. This case's global expansion route is a combination of multiple concrete assets: the Japan subsidiary, the Maison&Objet contract, and specialist partner firms for overseas buyer consultations.

**(5) A complete Section V structure**. What went into Section V as add-ons:
- LICORN investment support (5 winning teams × ₩10M)
- Overseas expo participation support (2 winning teams × ₩2M travel cost)
- Growth-support program linkage (the proposing firm's corporate incubation community)
- Global buyer consultation linkage

All sit at the intersection of two conditions — **"outside the RFP scope + the proposing firm's own asset"** AND **"directly contributes to the RFP's purpose."** They are not unrelated add-ons; they are add-ons that amplify the project's effect.

**(6) Key Value & Action structure**. The KPIs in this proposal run along two axes:
- **KEY VALUE**: building a region that leads global artisan culture by weaving the heritage of global artisans together (stated as a formal expression)
- **KEY ACTION**: through exchanges, festivals, and similar events, connecting local identity out to the world, and maximizing global interest and pride through project branding and PR

Splitting "value" and "action" into parallel tracks works well in B2G. Value alone is hollow; action alone is dry. Pairing them delivers the message that **both vision and execution are present**.

**(7) Caution — managing scale inflation**. Packing so many goals into a ₩1.32B budget carries **real risk**. Before a win becomes a contract, you must pre-check whether the targets are actually achievable. In particular:
- ₩100M global export target → Is there a real path to achieve it? (a Plan B beyond Maison&Objet)
- 10,000 expo visitors → Is the marketing budget allocation sufficient?
- 200 buyer consultations → Has the pre-invited buyer pool actually been secured?

If "we actually can't do it" surfaces after the win, it becomes fatal for the next-year renewal. When setting targets, **run your own internal validation one more time**.
