# 2025 Jongno-gu Seochon Local Brand Commercial District Strengthening — "머무는 온기, 서촌 (Lingering Warmth, Seochon)"

> Reading this case, you will see how a reusable parts kit — **"Commercial District Strengthening Body + merchant community + regional experience corps + branding action-learning + Relay Housewarming"** — was assembled for a local commerce project. User feedback points to this case as the one with the clearest, sharpest message.

## 1. Project One-Line Summary

- **Project Name**: 2025 Jongno-gu Seochon Local Brand Commercial District Strengthening — Operations Services Contract
- **Commissioning Entity**: Jongno-gu Office (B2G, local government)
- **Budget**: approx. KRW 539 million (VAT included)
- **Period**: contract date ~ December 31, 2025
- **Target Audience**: Seochon local-brand merchants, local residents, local creators, visitors
- **Core Tasks**:
  1. Seochon brand strengthening (local brand discovery, collaboration, consulting)
  2. Seochon coexistence-system building (Commercial District Strengthening Body, councils, administrative cooperation)
  3. Seochon local content development & operations (Seochon Brand Week, local pass)
  4. Seochon integrated PR and marketing

The "Local Brand Commercial District Cultivation Project" ran from 2022–2024; from 2025 it pivoted to **intensive cultivation of anchor stores within the district**. The context: this is the closing cohort of a three-stage arc — Stage 1 (vision setting, 2023) → Stage 2 (capacity building, 2024) → **Stage 3 (self-sustainability strengthening, 2025)**.

## 2. Key Messages

Original slogans:

> **"머무는 온기, 서촌" (Lingering Warmth, Seochon)**
> **"'머무는 장소'에서 '머물고 싶은 기억'으로" (From "a place to linger" to "a memory worth lingering in")**

The proposer laid out three interpretations:

1. Not merely a space, but a Seochon that leaves a warm feeling in every moment spent there.
2. A Seochon where local brands and creators go beyond business relationships and share warmth with one another.
3. A Seochon where residents and visitors share warm experiences and build lasting bonds.

The Korean concepts of 情 (jeong / deep affection), community, share, and empathy are condensed into a single phrase — "Lingering Warmth." Pairing an emotional message with a B2G local-government tone is rare. An emotional message alone easily reads as "sentimental but weak on execution," but here the five-layer chain and the three differentiation points below carry its weight.

## 3. Impact Value Chain — 5 Layers

- **Input**: KRW 539M budget; dedicated PM (executive level); 9 operating staff (group lead, executive lead, part lead, managers); merchant and resident network across the Seochon district; proprietary tools (brand-diagnosis tool, 6 Dimension Startup Growth Model); local-project experience across 93 cities/counties/districts; distribution-platform network including Idus and Ohou.
- **Activity**:
  - Commercial District Strengthening Body organization (merchant association, residents' council, external steering group, operations secretariat)
  - Branding action-learning (5-week intensive 1:1 coaching, local-brand workshop)
  - Relay Housewarming (monthly brand-collaboration program visiting themed stores in sequence)
  - "Seochon Homeowners" (merchant community, biweekly offline gathering)
  - "Seochon Chat Room" (regular resident–merchant communication program)
  - Regional experience corps in 4 types (residents, workers, external visitors, regulars)
  - Seochon Brand Week "Seochon Housewarming" (Oct 25 – Nov 2, large-scale festival)
  - Seochon local pass (paper + QR integrated pass, ~50 stores participating)
  - Integrated design development, PR and marketing (online and offline)
- **Output**:
  - 25+ pieces of local content developed
  - 9 large/small festivals, 10 regular community programs, 6 networking events
  - Commercial District Strengthening Body space built; community operations 4+ times
  - 20+ collaborative content pieces distributed (local creators and influencers)
  - 1 integrated commercial-district design guideline
- **Outcome**:
  - Stronger solidarity among merchants (Seochon Homeowners community transitions to self-operation)
  - Established communication channel between residents and merchants (Seochon Chat Room regularized)
  - Longer visitor dwell time (sequential visits driven by Relay Housewarming and local pass)
  - Revenue growth at individual stores (measured on Relay-participating stores)
  - Rent stabilization and shared operating space secured
- **Impact**:
  - The Commercial District Strengthening Body → graduates into a **Commercial District Management Body** from 2026 onward (organizational system, role division, operations manual handed over)
  - Seochon brand identity takes root (shared recognition among residents, merchants, and visitors)
  - Sustainable commercial-district ecosystem (merchant association can run regular events, PR, and community without an external operator)

## 4. Three Differentiation Points

### (1) Three-Tier Commercial District Strengthening Body + Stage-by-Stage Handover Roadmap

The Commercial District Strengthening Body is built as three tiers: (a) external steering group, (b) district councils (merchants and residents), (c) operations secretariat. Each party's role and decision-making path is spelled out, and the staged transition from **2025 (operator-led) → 2026 and beyond (merchant-association self-operated)** is concrete. The Stage 3 goal of "self-sustainability strengthening" is designed systemically.

Core elements per handover stage:
- Foundation-building for independence (representative selection, subcommittee formation)
- Strengthening the merchant association's internal systems (dispute mediation, regular meetings, data sharing, handover of the PR platform)
- Continued cooperation structure (external-network connections, follow-up advisory panel embedded for 2 months)

### (2) Seochon Homeowners · Seochon Chat Room · Regional Experience Corps — A Three-Type Community System

Not a generic "community program," but **three parallel communities with distinct purposes and participants**:

- **Seochon Homeowners** (merchant community): biweekly offline gathering; drives planning and operation of Relay Housewarming; incubates store-to-store collaboration content.
- **Seochon Chat Room** (open talk salon for residents + merchants): once a month; themed formats such as "Seochon living tips," "becoming a regular," "meet the owner."
- **Regional Experience Corps** (4 types): residents, workers, external visitors, and a regulars community (leveraging Seobapmo — "those who eat in Seochon"); collects real-user feedback on alley routes.

When this three-type structure is on the page, "resident participation plan," "merchant self-operation," and "external-visitor pull" are answered on the same page at the same time.

### (3) Relay Housewarming + Seochon Homeowners–Driven Planning + Local Pass Linkage

"Relay Housewarming" is a brand-collaboration program that brings visitors through a sequence of themed stores each month with benefits and participation events. The key elements:

- Merchants themselves plan the participating stores, theme, and benefits from within the **Seochon Homeowners** community.
- Runs May–September, twice a month (2nd and 3rd weekends).
- Invitation cards are distributed in a limited run (50 cards), linked to stamp-collection and prize events.
- Connects to the large-scale "Seochon Brand Week = Seochon Housewarming" in October (expanded via the Seochon local pass).
- Brand Week is organized in five sections: **Invitation (a seat where the heart lingers)** · **Gift (a handful of care)** · **Hospitality (only the heart is set)** · **Exchange (time spent together)** · **Memory (a snapshot of Seochon)**.

The Korean metaphor of "housewarming" runs consistently through the whole project, and merchants, residents, and visitors each have a role inside that metaphor.

## 5. How Underdogs' Proprietary Tools and Network Worked

- **Proprietary brand-diagnosis tool (6 Dimension–based)**: Used as the pre-diagnosis for the 5-week branding action-learning coaching. Measures the participating merchant's current stage on 6 axes and derives tailored coaching domains (space/interior, new-menu development, product/merchandise strategy, marketing, brand guide, revenue-expansion strategy).
- **Experience across 93 cities/counties/districts**: Used as quantitative-saturation evidence in the proposal — backing for the "nationwide local-based projects plus regional-centered ecosystem building" message.
- **Accumulated startup-coaching data**: 100,000+ records of local and founder growth data; 261 action-coaches nationwide (source figures). Provides the evidence base for Seochon merchant coaching.
- **Distribution network — Idus, Ohou, SSG, Lotte ON**: Offered as a Section V Bonus for connecting local brands to sales channels.
- **LICORN investment + LIPS loan matching**: Section V Bonus for early-stage production costs. KRW 20M+ of early-cost support plus up to 5× loan matching.
- **Global exhibition network (Maison&Objet, etc.)**: Proposed as a global expansion path for Seochon brands — the story being that even a local brand can go global.

## 6. Lessons for a New PM from This Case

**(1) Back an emotional message with a system.** "Lingering Warmth" is an emotional message, but behind it stands a tightly packed system — the Commercial District Strengthening Body, the three-type community, Relay Housewarming, and the handover roadmap. An emotional message alone is rated "hollow"; an emotional message supported by a system reads as **"this company has both feeling and execution power."** In local, commercial-district, and culture proposals, this combination is the strongest.

**(2) A reusable parts kit.** The elements of this proposal transplant cleanly to other local-commerce projects:

- Three-tier Commercial District Strengthening Body (external steering, council, secretariat)
- 5-week branding action-learning (1:1 on-site coaching + prototype development)
- Relay Housewarming (monthly course + invitation cards + prizes)
- Merchant community pattern "Seochon Homeowners" (biweekly gatherings)
- Resident–merchant talk salon pattern "Seochon Chat Room" (monthly)
- Regional experience corps in 4 types (residents, workers, externals, regulars)
- Brand Week festival in 5 sections (Invitation, Gift, Hospitality, Exchange, Memory)
- Local pass (paper + QR, 50 stores)

Swap the region name and store list and it becomes a draft for another local-government local-brand project. But **each region's emotional message must be built fresh** (no copy-paste of the original — see Ch.5 for the separate pitfall around this).

**(3) A handover roadmap is a value marker in B2G.** Local-government projects implicitly weight **"does it continue after the operator leaves?"** If you show a 2025 (operator-led) → 2026+ (merchant-association self-operated) roadmap up front, the commissioning entity reads it as "this company is not trying to prolong its own contract" — trust increases rather than decreases.

**(4) The causal chain is crisp.** The "So what?" test passes across Activity → Output → Outcome → Impact:
- We run Relay Housewarming → **Output**: monthly courses twice a month, 100 invitation cards distributed, 5 stores participating.
- So what? → **Outcome**: visitor dwell time grows, participating-store revenue rises, merchant-to-merchant collaboration network strengthens.
- So what? → **Impact**: the Seochon Homeowners community takes root → from 2026, the merchant association runs regular operations on its own.

"What state change does each activity create, and how does that change turn into system change?" — summarized in a single line — is the final strength of a local-project proposal.

**(5) Frame merchant-led planning as "capacity building," not "outsourcing."** Writing "merchants plan it themselves" carelessly reads as "the operator is pushing the work onto merchants." This proposal details concrete **operating procedures** for the Seochon Homeowners community (recruitment, PR, activity design, collaboration-content supply, offline-meeting operating allowances) and spells out the operator's progression through **planning-led → handover → advisory** stages. The tone is not "it'll run without us" but **"we're growing it together now so we can leave later."**

**(6) The role of integrated design.** "Design" is not decoration; it is **a tool of brand consistency**. The design ecosystem stretches from a resident-participation open call (photos, sketches, keyword suggestions) → local-artist selection → design development → goods extension → online/offline content templates. Local governments typically underfund "design line items," but when you explain that design is the spine of message delivery for the whole project, they read it differently.

**(7) Caution — scale sense.** Running this many programs on a KRW 539M budget requires **accurate staff mapping**. The original proposal includes a nine-person staffing table with detailed role division (overall project lead, planning lead, operations lead, external cooperation, PR and marketing, etc.). In local-project proposals, vague staff division draws the suspicion "the task list is big, but will it actually run?"
