## 6. Lessons for a New PM (general principles)

General principles that apply to global-track proposals even without access to the original:

**(1) The reality of an overseas entity or branch is an evaluation lever.** "We have a global network" and "Japan entity established in 2025" sit at very different points on the scoring scale. With its 2025 Japan and India entities, Underdogs secures a differentiator when bidding on global tracks.

**(2) On-site visits and MVP testing are not sightseeing.** When a proposal says "one on-site visit per team," it must be backed by concrete itineraries, visit targets, and test plans. An "overseas field trip" framing reads to evaluators as **"budget spending with uncertain effect."**

**(3) Global-mentor credibility.** Korean expats are often presented as "global mentors," but their local-market experience and network must be verifiable. Adding non-Korean mentors and on-the-ground entity staff into the mix raises credibility.

**(4) Section V regulars**:
- Support for global exhibitions such as Maison&Objet
- Overseas-buyer invitational consultations
- Use of overseas-entity (Japan, India) incubation space
- LICORN investment + LIPS loan matching up to 5×
- Placement on the global content platform (Asia Tomorrow)

**(5) Caution — realism of global KPIs.** KPIs like "10 teams expanding overseas, KRW 300M overseas revenue" can be excessive for a single-year aspiring-entrepreneur track. Confirm with the person in charge by phone what stage the commissioning entity actually expects (**preparation** for entry vs. actual **revenue**) before setting the KPI level.
