# Appendix A — Pre-submission Checklist

> Run this checklist the day before submission and on submission day itself. It is also worth running once right after the first draft is complete.

## How to Use

- [ ] Turn every item into a checkbox, then print it or copy it into Notion
- Work through each section **in order** (Structure → Content → Format)
- If any single item falls short, share it with the team immediately
- After submission, file this checklist in the case archive for reuse on the next bid

---

## 1. Structure Check

### Value Chain Consistency

- [ ] Confirm that the five layers — Input → Activity → Output → Outcome → Impact — are explicitly visible
- [ ] Verify that asking "so what?" between layers produces an answer
- [ ] Confirm that Output and Outcome are not conflated
- [ ] Verify that Outcome figures are supported by evidence (cases, diagnostic data)
- [ ] Confirm that Impact claims stay within this project's realistic contribution boundary

### Scoring-Weight Strategy

- [ ] Confirm that you have the RFP evaluation rubric with per-item point weights
- [ ] Verify that page distribution across the proposal is roughly proportional to the scoring weights
- [ ] Confirm that the heaviest section corresponds to the highest-scoring item
- [ ] Verify that low-weight items are not over-written
- [ ] Confirm that bonus-point, deduction, and mandatory-submission clauses have not been missed

### Activity / Outcome Causality

- [ ] Verify that every major Activity has at least one expected Outcome attached
- [ ] Confirm that the same Activity is described consistently when referenced across sections
- [ ] Verify that each curriculum session specifies deliverables, tools, and time allocation
- [ ] Confirm that no three consecutive theory sessions appear without an Action Week inserted (Action Week is mandatory beyond three consecutive theory sessions)

---

## 2. Content Check

### Quantitative Specificity

- [ ] Ctrl+F the words `many`, `various`, `sufficient`, `abundant`, `optimal` (and their Korean equivalents)
- [ ] Replace every vague expression found with either a **number** or a **concrete noun**
- [ ] Verify that every claim carries at least one piece of evidence (figure, case, tool, system)
- [ ] Confirm that quantitative saturation is spread across the document rather than concentrated in one section

### Proprietary Tool-Name Accuracy

- [ ] **ACT-PRENEURSHIP** (exact hyphenation and capitalization)
- [ ] **DOGS Team Building** (DOGS uppercase)
- [ ] **IMPACT Startup Methodology** (IMPACT uppercase + official "6 stages, 18 modules, 54 questions" notation)
- [ ] **6 Dimension Startup Growth Model**
- [ ] **EduBot** (capital E and B)
- [ ] **Underbase LMS**
- [ ] **LICORN** — Lifestyle & Local Innovation uniCORN
- [ ] **Asia Tomorrow** (platform, launched 2024)
- [ ] Verify that no proprietary tool name is altered, abbreviated, or lowercased anywhere in the proposal

### 4-Tier Support System Notation

- [ ] Confirm that coaches/mentors are not described as a **single role** (no "single coach" framing)
- [ ] Verify that all four layers appear: expert mentor pool + consultant pool + dedicated coach (action coach) + peer network
- [ ] Confirm that AI Coach (EduBot) is **not** presented as a separate tier or standalone product (mention only as a strengthening tool)

### Section V Bonus

- [ ] Confirm that three to four out-of-RFP-scope additional proposals are included
- [ ] Verify that each add-on sits at the intersection of **proposer's proprietary asset × contribution to RFP purpose**
- [ ] Confirm that one to two items cover global / impact report / follow-on investment / post-program community / archiving
- [ ] Verify that every Section V item lists a **concrete, feasible pathway** (specific expo name, platform, partner)

### Brand Messaging

- [ ] Confirm that the operating-entity name is consistently rendered as "Underdogs" (use "UD Impact Co., Ltd." only in legal sections)
- [ ] Verify that the word "underdog" appears in its redefined sense, never in an "underprivileged / pity" frame
- [ ] Confirm that the mission, vision, and tagline are quoted verbatim at the appropriate places
- [ ] Verify that the Core Values (Collaboration · Innovation · Performance · Responsibility) appear in the organization / team introduction section

---

## 3. Format Check

### Typos and Broken Sentences

- [ ] Proposer name, funder name, and contact person name — no typos
- [ ] Project name, program name, and figures — no typos
- [ ] Korean/English parallel notation is consistent (e.g., 액트프러너(Act-Preneur), K-souvenir)
- [ ] Spacing rules around English letters, numbers, and symbols are consistent
- [ ] Repetitive expressions (e.g., "conducted", "operated") have been given a final pass

### File and Design Alignment

- [ ] File name follows the RFP's naming rule
- [ ] File format (PDF / HWP / PPT) matches what the RFP requires
- [ ] File size is within the limit
- [ ] Page count is within the limit (verify whether body and appendix are counted together)
- [ ] Font family is unified and font sizes are consistent
- [ ] Images, tables, and charts are properly aligned
- [ ] Page numbers, headers, and footers are consistent
- [ ] Internal links and table-of-contents navigation work correctly

### Year Values

- [ ] Year notation is consistent (e.g., 2025, 2024) — watch for mixed relative expressions like "next year", "this year", "last year"
- [ ] Cumulative performance figures reflect the latest values (source of truth: `src/lib/ud-brand.ts` — see [Appendix B](./b-ud-assets.md))
- [ ] Year, amount, and participant counts in past-case references are accurate

### Submission Information

- [ ] Submission deadline and method (online / in-person / postal) are confirmed
- [ ] Mandatory supporting documents (business registration certificate, financial statements, performance bond, corporate seal, etc.) are ready
- [ ] Submission contact person and phone number are recorded correctly
- [ ] Backup file is stored on a separate drive or email account

---

## 4. Funder-Type-Specific Checks (see Ch.10)

### If B2G

- [ ] Confirm that language is not excessively innovative or experimental in tone
- [ ] Verify that B2B vocabulary (revenue, ROI, profit maximization) has not crept in
- [ ] Confirm that the safety, reporting, personal data protection, and risk sections together run at least one page
- [ ] Verify that performance indicators are in a format usable for government performance evaluation and audit

### If B2B

- [ ] Confirm that government-program vocabulary (implementation framework, self-sustainability, etc.) has not crept in
- [ ] Verify that business KPIs (revenue, conversion rate, repeat-participation rate) sit at the center
- [ ] Confirm that sustainability report / ESG report format delivery is promised
- [ ] Verify that a re-contracting structure (expansion path for next year and the year after) is implied

### If Renewal

- [ ] Confirm that all "nice to meet you" tone has been removed
- [ ] Verify that last year's performance summary plus this year's improvement plan appears early
- [ ] Confirm that KPIs have been upgraded versus last year
- [ ] Verify that concrete improvement plans address last year's weak points
- [ ] Confirm that no section is a verbatim copy-paste from last year's proposal

---

## 5. Team Sign-off and Final Approval

- [ ] PM, division head, and CEO have reviewed
- [ ] Finance, accounting, and legal items are confirmed (budget execution, performance bond, contract clauses)
- [ ] External expert feedback (if needed) has been incorporated
- [ ] A minimum **24-hour cooling-off period** has been secured before submission (overnight submissions produce a spike in typos and broken sentences)

---

## One Final Sentence

Close the proposal and ask yourself one question:

> **"Will an evaluator who does not know this company, reading only this proposal, come away convinced that (1) we read the RFP accurately, (2) we answer with evidence, and (3) we have the capacity to execute?"**

If the answer is "yes" on all three, submit. If even one gives pause, revisit that section.
